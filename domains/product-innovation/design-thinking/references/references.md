# Design Thinking — References

## Foundational Works
- Tim Brown: *Change by Design* (2009) — Defined design thinking as a human-centered innovation methodology and established IDEO's approach of empathy, ideation, and prototyping for business problem-solving.
- Herbert A. Simon: *The Sciences of the Artificial* (1969) — Provided the intellectual foundation for design as a discipline of problem-solving, introducing the concept of designing as a way of transforming existing into preferred situations.
- Nigel Cross: *Design Thinking: Understanding How Designers Think and Work* (2011) — Synthesized decades of design cognition research to characterize how expert designers frame problems and generate solutions differently from scientists and engineers.

## Academic Sources
- Roger Martin: *The Design of Business: Why Design Thinking is the Next Competitive Advantage*, Harvard Business Press (2009) — Argued that companies excelling at both analytical thinking and intuitive design thinking achieve sustained competitive advantage.
- Jeanne Liedtka: *Perspective: Linking Design Thinking with Innovation Outcomes through Cognitive Mechanisms*, Product Development and Management Association (2015) — Identified the cognitive mechanisms by which design thinking practices produce novel and viable innovation outcomes.

## Practitioner Resources
- IDEO: *The Field Guide to Human-Centered Design* (2015) — Practical methods handbook covering the full human-centered design process with step-by-step facilitation guides for empathy, ideation, and prototyping activities.
- Stanford d.school (Hasso Plattner Institute of Design): *Design Thinking Bootleg* — Open-source collection of methods and mindsets from Stanford's design school codifying the five-stage Empathize-Define-Ideate-Prototype-Test framework.
