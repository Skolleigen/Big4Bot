# Lean Startup Cycle — References

## Foundational Works
- Eric Ries: *The Lean Startup* (2011) — Introduced the Build-Measure-Learn feedback loop and validated learning methodology for reducing waste and accelerating product development in conditions of extreme uncertainty.
- Steve Blank & Bob Dorf: *The Startup Owner's Manual* (2012) — Provided the definitive step-by-step guide to customer development methodology, detailing how to get out of the building and validate hypotheses before scaling.
- Steve Blank: *The Four Steps to the Epiphany* (2005) — Originated the customer development framework that underpins the Lean Startup, distinguishing customer discovery and validation from company building.

## Academic Sources
- Frank Robinson: *Minimum Viable Product*, SyncDev Inc. (2001) — Credited as the originator of the MVP concept, defining it as the version of a product that maximizes return on risk for both the vendor and the customer.
- Blank, S.: *Why the Lean Start-Up Changes Everything*, Harvard Business Review (2013) — Synthesized how customer development, agile development, and business model design combine to replace the traditional product launch plan.

## Practitioner Resources
- Ash Maurya: *Running Lean* (O'Reilly, 2012) — Practical guide adapting the Lean Startup methodology with the Lean Canvas tool, providing a structured process for iterating from Plan A to a plan that works.
- IDEO & SVPG: *Lean Product and Process Development Resources* — Practitioner frameworks and case libraries documenting real-world applications of MVP testing, pivot decisions, and innovation accounting across industries.
