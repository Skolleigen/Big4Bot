# Value Proposition Canvas — References

## Foundational Works
- Alexander Osterwalder, Yves Pigneur, Greg Bernarda & Alan Smith: *Value Proposition Design* (2014) — Introduced the Value Proposition Canvas as a tool for mapping the fit between customer profiles (jobs, pains, gains) and value maps (products, pain relievers, gain creators).
- Alexander Osterwalder & Yves Pigneur: *Business Model Generation* (2010) — Established the Business Model Canvas within which the Value Proposition building block is the nucleus, providing the parent framework for the Value Proposition Canvas.
- Geoffrey Moore: *Crossing the Chasm* (1991) — Provided the positioning statement format and technology adoption lifecycle model that inform how value propositions must be tailored for different customer segments at different stages of market adoption.

## Academic Sources
- Osterwalder, A. et al.: *An Entrepreneur's Guide to the Value Proposition Canvas*, Harvard Business Review (2015) — Explained how to apply the canvas in practice to test value proposition hypotheses against real customer evidence before committing to product development.
- Christoph Zott & Raphael Amit: *The Fit Between Product Market Strategy and Business Model: Implications for Firm Performance*, Strategic Management Journal (2008) — Demonstrated empirically that alignment between value proposition and business model design significantly improves firm performance.

## Practitioner Resources
- Strategyzer: *Value Proposition Canvas Resources and Testing Guides* — Official Strategyzer platform documentation, video tutorials, and testing checklists for applying the Value Proposition Canvas through structured customer interviews and assumption testing.
- Clayton Christensen Institute: *Jobs to Be Done Resources* — Framework and case study library on the jobs-to-be-done theory, which provides the analytical lens for identifying the customer "jobs" that anchor a compelling value proposition.
