# Value Proposition Canvas

## Domain
Product & Innovation

## Description
Tool for designing products and services that precisely match customer needs by mapping the relationship between customer jobs, pains, and gains against product features, pain relievers, and gain creators.

## Purpose
The Value Proposition Canvas makes the fit — or misfit — between a product and its target customer explicit and actionable. It forces teams to articulate customer needs in the customer's own terms before mapping product capabilities to those needs, preventing the common failure mode of building features justified by internal logic rather than validated customer demand. The canvas produces a structured basis for roadmap prioritization, messaging development, and competitive differentiation analysis.

## When to Use
- A product team is designing a new offering and needs to validate that planned features address real customer needs
- An existing product is experiencing adoption or retention problems and the team needs to diagnose whether the value proposition is misaligned with what customers actually need
- A messaging or positioning refresh requires grounding in documented customer jobs, pains, and gains
- Competitive positioning analysis requires a structured comparison of how competing products address the same customer profile

## When NOT to Use
- The customer segment and their jobs have already been extensively researched and validated recently; proceed directly to solution design or roadmap planning
- The engagement is focused on financial or operational analysis where value proposition design is not the primary variable
- The product serves a regulatory or compliance function where customer preference does not drive adoption decisions
- The team lacks access to actual customers for research to populate the customer profile with primary data

## Inputs Required
- Defined target customer segment with demographic and behavioral characteristics
- Customer research data: interview transcripts, observation notes, or survey results capturing jobs, pains, and gains
- Inventory of existing or proposed product features, services, and capabilities
- Prioritization input on which customer jobs are most important to the segment

## Optional Inputs
- Competitive product feature maps to identify where competitors address the same customer profile
- Net Promoter Score verbatims or support ticket logs to surface unarticulated pains

## Diagnostic Process
1. Define the target customer segment with enough specificity to distinguish it from adjacent segments; document the segment's context, role, and relevant behavioral characteristics.
2. Map customer jobs by identifying functional jobs (tasks to accomplish), social jobs (how the customer wants to be perceived), and emotional jobs (how the customer wants to feel) relevant to the domain addressed by the product.
3. Document customer pains in the customer's language: obstacles that prevent job completion, risks of negative outcomes, and sources of friction or frustration currently experienced; rank pains by severity from the customer's perspective.
4. Document customer gains as the outcomes and benefits customers require, expect, desire, or would be surprised by; rank gains by relevance, distinguishing essential gains from nice-to-have ones.
5. Map the product's features and services against the completed customer profile, listing each distinct capability without yet evaluating fit.
6. Identify pain relievers: for each significant customer pain, assess whether a specific product feature directly reduces or eliminates that pain; note where pains are unaddressed by current capabilities.
7. Identify gain creators: for each relevant customer gain, assess whether a specific product feature produces or enhances that outcome; note where valued gains are not yet created by the product.
8. Assess fit between the customer profile and the value map by scoring how completely the product addresses the most important jobs, relieves the most severe pains, and creates the most valued gains; document fit gaps as explicit roadmap or positioning inputs.

## Output
The output is a completed value proposition canvas with a fit assessment that identifies matched and unmatched elements between the customer profile and the value map. The deliverable includes a prioritized list of fit gaps — ranked by customer importance — that directly inform product roadmap decisions, messaging strategy, and investment prioritization.
