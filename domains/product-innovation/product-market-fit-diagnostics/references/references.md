# Product-Market Fit Diagnostics — References

## Foundational Works
- Marc Andreessen: *The Only Thing That Matters* (2007) — Coined and popularized the term "product-market fit," arguing it is the single most important milestone for an early-stage startup and the primary factor separating successful from failed ventures.
- Dan Olsen: *The Lean Product Playbook* (2015) — Provided a systematic six-step process for achieving product-market fit, including target customer definition, underserved needs identification, and iterative value proposition testing.
- Sean Ellis & Morgan Brown: *Hacking Growth* (2017) — Documented Ellis's product-market fit survey methodology and the 40% benchmark threshold indicating sufficient fit for growth investment.

## Academic Sources
- Andrew Chen: *The Red Flags and Magic Numbers That Investors Look for in Your Startup's Metrics*, Andreessen Horowitz (2012) — Analyzed retention curve shapes and engagement metrics as diagnostic signals for product-market fit strength in consumer products.
- Rahul Vohra: *How Superhuman Built an Engine to Find Product-Market Fit*, First Round Review (2018) — Presented a quantitative scoring framework extending the Ellis survey methodology to segment users and systematically improve fit over time.

## Practitioner Resources
- Sean Ellis: *Startup-Marketing.com / GoPractice PMF Survey Methodology* — Original documentation of the "how disappointed" survey instrument and the 40% threshold benchmark used to assess product-market fit readiness for growth.
- Y Combinator: *Product-Market Fit Resources and Partner Advice* — Curated guidance from YC partners and alumni on recognizing, measuring, and iterating toward product-market fit across B2B and B2C product categories.
