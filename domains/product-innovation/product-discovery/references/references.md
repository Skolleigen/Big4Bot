# Product Discovery — References

## Foundational Works
- Teresa Torres: *Continuous Discovery Habits* (2021) — Established a structured approach to ongoing product discovery using opportunity solution trees, continuous interviewing, and assumption testing to replace episodic research.
- Marty Cagan: *Inspired: How to Create Tech Products Customers Love* (2017) — Defined the role of modern product management and the discovery process used by leading technology companies to identify and validate product opportunities before delivery.
- Jeff Gothelf & Josh Seiden: *Lean UX: Designing Great Products with Agile Teams* (2016) — Integrated UX research and design directly into agile development cycles, advocating for assumption-driven discovery and collaborative cross-functional experimentation.

## Academic Sources
- Jake Knapp, John Zeratsky & Braden Kowitz: *Sprint: How to Solve Big Problems and Test New Ideas in Just Five Days*, Simon & Schuster (2016) — Documented Google Ventures' five-day design sprint process as a time-boxed discovery and prototyping methodology for de-risking product decisions.
- Ulwick, Anthony W.: *Jobs-to-Be-Done: Theory to Practice*, IDEA Bite Press (2016) — Formalized the jobs-to-be-done framework as a rigorous discovery lens for understanding the functional, emotional, and social outcomes customers seek.

## Practitioner Resources
- Silicon Valley Product Group (SVPG): *Product Discovery Articles and Resources* — Practitioner essays and frameworks by Marty Cagan and colleagues covering continuous discovery, dual-track agile, and product team empowerment practices.
- ProductBoard & Amplitude: *Product Discovery Playbooks* — Industry tooling documentation and benchmark reports on how modern product teams structure discovery cadences, prioritize opportunities, and measure discovery outcomes.
