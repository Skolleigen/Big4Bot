# Business Model Canvas

## Domain
Product & Innovation

## Description
Strategic management tool for describing, designing, and analyzing business models across nine building blocks: customer segments, value propositions, channels, customer relationships, revenue streams, key resources, key activities, key partnerships, and cost structure.

## Purpose
The Business Model Canvas provides a shared visual language for articulating how an organization creates, delivers, and captures value. It enables teams to evaluate the internal coherence of a business model and identify gaps or misalignments before committing capital. The canvas serves as a baseline for comparing current-state models against proposed alternatives or competitor configurations.

## When to Use
- A new venture or product line requires a structured model design before investment decisions are made
- An existing business unit is underperforming and the root cause may be a structural model flaw
- A client is evaluating a strategic pivot or entering an adjacent market
- Comparing business model alternatives to select the most viable configuration

## When NOT to Use
- A single operational problem (e.g., pricing or distribution) is already well-defined and scoped; use a targeted framework instead
- The engagement is limited to financial modeling without strategic redesign
- The organization lacks leadership access needed to validate key assumptions across all nine blocks
- The customer segment has not yet been validated and full model design would be premature

## Inputs Required
- Description of the target customer segments served or proposed
- Current or proposed value proposition for each segment
- Existing revenue and cost data or reliable estimates
- Organizational capability inventory covering resources, activities, and key partnerships

## Optional Inputs
- Competitor business model profiles for benchmarking
- Customer research data or Jobs-to-be-Done analysis to sharpen value proposition articulation

## Diagnostic Process
1. Define customer segments by grouping customers with shared needs, behaviors, or demographics into distinct, named cohorts with estimated sizes.
2. Articulate the value proposition for each segment, specifying which customer problems are solved, which gains are created, and how the offering differs from alternatives.
3. Map distribution and communication channels through which each segment is reached, sold to, and supported, noting ownership (own vs. partner) and cost per channel.
4. Define the customer relationship model for each segment, specifying whether interactions are self-serve, dedicated account management, automated, community-based, or co-creation.
5. Identify all revenue streams, noting pricing mechanisms (fixed, dynamic, auction, subscription), revenue type (transactional, recurring, licensing, usage-based), and relative contribution to total revenue.
6. List key resources required to deliver the value proposition, categorizing each as physical, intellectual, human, or financial.
7. Define key activities the organization must perform to operate the model, deliver value, maintain channels, and sustain customer relationships.
8. Identify key partnerships and the strategic rationale for each: resource acquisition, risk reduction, cost optimization, or outsourcing of non-core activities.
9. Map the full cost structure by linking costs to specific activities and resources, distinguishing fixed from variable costs and identifying the three largest cost drivers.
10. Analyze model coherence and viability by testing whether resources, activities, and partnerships are sufficient to deliver each value proposition, and whether projected revenue streams justify the cost structure at target volume.

## Output
The output is a completed nine-block canvas with an accompanying coherence assessment that identifies gaps, misalignments, and prioritized redesign opportunities. Each block contains specific, verifiable entries rather than generic statements, enabling the client to use the canvas as an actionable strategic artifact and input to subsequent financial modeling.
