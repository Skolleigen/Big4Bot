# Business Model Canvas — References

## Foundational Works
- Alexander Osterwalder & Yves Pigneur: *Business Model Generation* (2010) — Introduced the Business Model Canvas as a strategic management tool for describing, designing, and pivoting business models across nine building blocks.
- Alexander Osterwalder, Yves Pigneur, Greg Bernarda & Alan Smith: *Value Proposition Design* (2014) — Extended the Canvas framework with the Value Proposition Canvas to map customer jobs, pains, gains, and corresponding product/service fit.
- Henry Chesbrough: *Open Business Models* (2006) — Argued that firms can create and capture more value by incorporating external ideas and paths to market into their business model design.

## Academic Sources
- Alexander Osterwalder: *The Business Model Ontology — A Proposition in a Design Science Approach*, University of Lausanne (2004) — Doctoral dissertation that provided the theoretical foundation and ontological structure underpinning the Business Model Canvas.
- Christoph Zott & Raphael Amit: *Business Model Design and the Performance of Entrepreneurial Firms*, Organization Science (2007) — Empirically examined how activity-system design choices in business models affect new venture performance.

## Practitioner Resources
- Strategyzer: *Strategyzer Platform and Canvas Templates* — Official platform by Osterwalder's firm offering digital Canvas tools, tutorials, testing methodologies, and case studies for practitioners.
- Steve Blank: *Why the Lean Start-Up Changes Everything*, Harvard Business Review (2013) — Connected Business Model Canvas usage to customer development and lean experimentation practices for early-stage ventures.
