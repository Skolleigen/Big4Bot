# Competitive Positioning — References

## Foundational Works
- Al Ries & Jack Trout: *Positioning: The Battle for Your Mind* (1981) — Seminal work establishing positioning as a mental battle for customer perception; introduced the concept of "owning a word" in the prospect's mind
- Michael E. Porter: *Competitive Strategy: Techniques for Analyzing Industries and Competitors* (1980) — Framework for competitive advantage through cost leadership, differentiation, and focus strategies; foundation for strategic positioning analysis
- Michael Treacy & Fred Wiersema: *The Discipline of Market Leaders* (1995) — Identifies three value disciplines (operational excellence, product leadership, customer intimacy) and argues firms must excel at one while meeting threshold standards in others
- W. Chan Kim & Renée Mauborgne: *Blue Ocean Strategy* (2004) — Framework for creating uncontested market space by reconstructing market boundaries rather than competing in existing ones; introduces the Strategy Canvas tool

## Academic Sources
- Pankaj Ghemawat: *Commitment: The Dynamic of Strategy*, Harvard Business Review Press — Examines how commitment to a strategic position creates sustainable competitive advantage over time
- David Aaker & Gary Shansby: *Positioning Your Product*, Business Horizons (1982) — Landmark academic treatment of positioning dimensions including product attributes, use/application, user category, and competitor-based positioning
- Kevin Lane Keller: *Conceptualizing, Measuring, and Managing Customer-Based Brand Equity*, Journal of Marketing (1993) — Provides the CBBE framework linking brand positioning to customer knowledge structures and long-term equity
- George Day & Robin Wensley: *Assessing Advantage: A Framework for Diagnosing Competitive Superiority*, Journal of Marketing (1988) — Analytical framework for diagnosing sources of competitive advantage and translating positional superiority into performance

## Practitioner Resources
- McKinsey & Company: *The Art of Strategy* (McKinsey Quarterly) — Practitioner perspective on aligning positioning with corporate strategy and capabilities
- Gartner: *Magic Quadrant Methodology* — Industry-standard competitive landscape visualization framework widely used in B2B technology markets; defines Leaders, Challengers, Visionaries, and Niche Players
- Simon-Kucher & Partners: *Positioning and Pricing Alignment Research* — Practitioner research linking positioning choices to willingness-to-pay and monetization strategy
- April Dunford: *Obviously Awesome: How to Nail Product Positioning so Customers Get It, Buy It, Love It* (2019) — Modern practitioner guide to B2B product positioning with a structured 10-step process applicable to SaaS and technology companies
