# Competitive Positioning — Example

## Scenario
Meridian HCM is a regional HR software vendor with $35M ARR serving mid-market companies (100–2,000 employees). A new low-cost entrant (ZenHR) has undercut Meridian's pricing by 40%, while Workday continues to win enterprise deals above 2,000 employees. Customer win/loss analysis shows Meridian losing 6 of 10 competitive deals, with reps unable to articulate a differentiated value proposition.

## Inputs
- **Company:** Meridian HCM ($35M ARR, 320 customers, avg ACV $109K)
- **Competitive set:** ZenHR (low-cost), BambooHR (SMB), Rippling (tech-forward SMB), Workday (enterprise), SAP SuccessFactors (enterprise), Paylocity (mid-market)
- **Positioning axes:** Price tier (low → premium) vs. feature depth (basic HR → full HCM suite); target segment (SMB <200 → Enterprise 2,000+)

## Analysis

**Step 1 — Competitor identification and segmentation:**
Six competitors mapped across two axes. ZenHR and BambooHR cluster in the low-price/SMB quadrant. Workday and SAP SuccessFactors occupy the high-price/enterprise quadrant. Rippling sits at mid-price/SMB. Paylocity occupies mid-price/mid-market. Meridian currently overlaps heavily with Paylocity with no clear differentiation on either axis.

**Step 2 — Positioning map construction:**
Plotting all six competitors on price vs. feature depth reveals an underserved white space: mid-market companies (500–2,000 employees) in regulated industries (healthcare, financial services, manufacturing) that require compliance-grade HR tooling but cannot afford Workday implementation costs ($500K+). No incumbent currently owns a "compliance-first mid-market HCM" position.

**Step 3 — Customer interview validation:**
Ten structured interviews with existing Meridian customers in healthcare and financial services confirm the hypothesis. Eight of ten cite "compliance reporting and audit trail" as their top evaluation criterion — a capability Meridian possesses but does not actively market. None cited price as the primary driver when compliance risk was present.

**Step 4 — White space definition:**
Target segment refined to: companies with 500–2,000 employees in regulated industries (HIPAA, SOX, state-level labor compliance) seeking a mid-market HCM with enterprise-grade compliance audit trails, pre-built regulatory reports, and dedicated implementation support at 60% of Workday's total cost of ownership.

**Step 5 — Positioning statement drafting:**
Draft positioning statement: "For HR leaders at regulated mid-market companies who cannot afford the complexity of enterprise HCM but cannot accept the compliance risk of basic HR tools, Meridian HCM is the only purpose-built compliance-first HCM platform that delivers audit-ready regulatory reporting and dedicated implementation support at a predictable mid-market price."

**Step 6 — Competitive messaging framework:**
Three against-competitor battle cards developed. vs. ZenHR: total compliance risk cost (healthcare audit failures average $2.3M). vs. Paylocity: pre-built HIPAA/SOX reports vs. custom configuration required. vs. Workday: 8-week implementation vs. 18-month enterprise rollout.
