# Customer Journey Mapping — References

## Foundational Works
- Adam Richardson: *Using Customer Journey Maps to Improve Customer Experience*, Harvard Business Review (2010) — Practitioner-oriented introduction to journey mapping as a diagnostic tool; established the framework's application in B2B and service businesses
- IDEO: *The Field Guide to Human-Centered Design* (2015) — Design thinking methodology that underpins journey mapping practice; covers empathy research, journey visualization, and iterative prototyping
- Chip Heath & Dan Heath: *The Power of Moments* (2017) — Examines how defining moments shape customer perception disproportionately; provides theoretical grounding for identifying and engineering high-impact touchpoints

## Academic Sources
- Mark S. Rosenbaum, Mauricio L. Otalora & German C. Ramirez: *How to Create a Realistic Customer Journey Map*, Business Horizons, Vol. 60, Issue 1 (2017) — Peer-reviewed methodology for constructing journey maps that accurately reflect customer experience rather than internal process assumptions
- Katherine N. Lemon & Peter C. Verhoef: *Understanding Customer Experience Throughout the Customer Journey*, Journal of Marketing, Vol. 80, No. 6 (2016) — Comprehensive academic framework integrating customer experience management across pre-purchase, purchase, and post-purchase stages; widely cited for touchpoint categorization
- Arne De Keyser, Katherine N. Lemon, Phil Klaus & Timothy L. Keiningham: *A Framework for Understanding and Managing the Customer Experience*, Marketing Science Institute Working Paper (2015) — Extends journey mapping to include contextual, relational, and situational factors affecting customer experience
- Paul Hagen: *The Evolution of Journey Mapping*, Forrester Research (2019) — Documents the transition from static journey maps to dynamic, data-driven experience management systems

## Practitioner Resources
- Forrester Research: *The Customer Journey Mapping Best Practices Report* — Annual practitioner benchmark covering journey map construction, validation methods, and organizational integration; includes B2B-specific guidance
- Nielsen Norman Group: *Journey Mapping 101* and *When and How to Create Customer Journey Maps* — Foundational UX practitioner guidance on research methods, visualization formats, and common mapping errors
- Salesforce Research: *State of the Connected Customer* (annual) — Quantitative benchmark data on customer expectations across touchpoints; useful for establishing baseline expectations during friction point analysis
- Jeanne Bliss: *Chief Customer Officer 2.0* (2015) — Practitioner framework for embedding journey thinking into organizational accountability structures; addresses the internal owner mapping challenge
