# Customer Journey Mapping — Example

## Scenario
Pinnacle Risk Advisors is a B2B commercial insurance broker with $120M in annual premiums under management, serving mid-market companies across manufacturing, logistics, and professional services. Despite strong new business acquisition, the firm is experiencing 38% client churn at annual renewal — well above the industry average of 18%. Exit interviews point to dissatisfaction during the quote and renewal phases, but the specific friction points have not been systematically documented.

## Inputs
- **Primary persona:** Sarah Chen, CFO at a 450-person manufacturing company; manages a $380K annual insurance program; renewal decision-maker; time-constrained, risk-averse
- **Journey scope:** Six stages from initial awareness through advocacy — Awareness, Quote, Bind, Manage, Renew, Advocate
- **Data sources:** 22 client exit interviews, NPS survey results (score: 31), CRM interaction logs (18 months), internal process documentation from underwriting and account management teams

## Analysis

**Step 1 — Persona definition:**
Sarah Chen profile confirmed through 12 in-depth interviews. Key characteristics: makes renewal decision jointly with Risk Manager; primary concern is program adequacy (not lowest price); expects proactive communication; has 3–4 competing broker relationships; switches brokers when she feels "forgotten between renewals." Jobs-to-be-done: protect balance sheet, simplify compliance documentation, avoid renewal surprises.

**Step 2 — Journey stage mapping:**
Six stages identified: Awareness (recognizing coverage gaps or dissatisfaction with incumbent), Quote (submitting application, receiving proposals), Bind (finalizing coverage, signing documents), Manage (mid-year certificates, endorsements, claims), Renew (90-day pre-renewal through policy effective date), Advocate (referrals and case study participation). Each stage mapped with touchpoints, channels, and internal owners.

**Step 3 — Touchpoint inventory:**
Forty-one touchpoints identified across six stages. Awareness: 4 touchpoints (referral, event, digital search, cold outreach). Quote: 9 touchpoints (submission, underwriting Q&A, proposal delivery, comparison review, pricing discussion). Bind: 6 touchpoints. Manage: 14 touchpoints (heaviest volume — certificates, endorsements, mid-year reviews). Renew: 6 touchpoints. Advocate: 2 touchpoints.

**Step 4 — Emotion mapping and pain point identification:**
Client emotion plotted at each touchpoint using exit interview data. Three critical friction moments identified: (1) Quote stage — opaque pricing with no explanation of rate drivers; clients feel "handed a number with no context," leading to distrust. (2) Bind stage — document processing averages 11 days vs. client expectation of 3 days; 4 of 22 exit clients cited a missed contract deadline caused by binding delays. (3) Renew stage — first proactive outreach from broker occurs average 34 days before renewal vs. client expectation of 90 days; clients feel the relationship is transactional.

**Step 5 — Internal owner mapping:**
Each touchpoint mapped to an internal owner. Quote friction owned by Account Management (pricing transparency gap) and Underwriting (slow response). Bind friction owned by Operations (document processing SLA). Renewal friction owned by Account Management (CRM-based outreach cadence not enforced). No single owner is accountable for the end-to-end renewal experience.

**Step 6 — Prioritization of improvements:**
Three improvements prioritized using impact/effort scoring: (1) Introduce "pricing rationale" one-pager delivered with every proposal — high impact, low effort (template-based). (2) Reduce bind document processing from 11 days to 3 days via workflow automation — high impact, medium effort (operations project). (3) Enforce 90-day renewal outreach cadence via CRM automation — high impact, low effort (CRM configuration). Secondary improvement: assign dedicated renewal quarterback per account above $100K premium.
