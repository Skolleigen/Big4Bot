# Inbound Marketing Strategy — Example

## Scenario
Vantage Cyber is a 45-person cybersecurity consulting firm generating $8.2M in annual revenue, with 80% of revenue sourced from partner referrals and two anchor client relationships. Leadership wants to build a scalable inbound pipeline targeting CISOs and VP-level security leaders at companies with 500–5,000 employees in financial services and healthcare verticals. The firm has no dedicated marketing function and a content library consisting of two blog posts (last updated 14 months ago) and one technical whitepaper.

## Inputs
- **Primary persona:** Marcus Webb, CISO at a 1,800-person regional bank; budget authority for a $1.2M annual security program; evaluates vendors through peer recommendations and thought leadership; attends RSA Conference and ISC2 events annually
- **Revenue target:** $2M in inbound-sourced pipeline within 18 months
- **Current content audit:** 2 blog posts (technical, no SEO optimization), 1 whitepaper (gated, 47 downloads total), 0 case studies, 0 comparison guides, 0 ROI calculators, no email nurture sequences

## Analysis

**Step 1 — Persona and buyer journey definition:**
Marcus Webb persona validated through 8 CISO interviews. Buyer journey mapped across three stages: Awareness ("Are we compliant with the new SEC cybersecurity disclosure rules?"), Consideration ("Which framework — SOC 2, NIST CSF, or ISO 27001 — fits our risk profile and regulatory environment?"), Decision ("Which partner has demonstrated expertise with banks our size and can deliver within our budget?"). Content gap analysis performed at each stage.

**Step 2 — Content gap analysis:**
Awareness stage: zero educational content on SEC disclosure rules, emerging threat briefings, or compliance deadline explainers — highest-traffic search intent category for target persona. Consideration stage: no framework comparison guides, no "NIST CSF vs. SOC 2" content, no maturity assessment tools. Decision stage: no case studies with named clients, no ROI calculator, no vendor selection guide. Existing whitepaper is Decision-stage content with no Awareness or Consideration content feeding it.

**Step 3 — 12-month content plan construction:**
Content plan structured around 4 content pillars: Regulatory Compliance (SEC, HIPAA, SOC 2), Threat Intelligence (ransomware, supply chain, insider threats), Framework Guidance (NIST CSF, ISO 27001 implementation), and Vendor Selection (evaluation criteria, RFP templates). Month 1–3: 6 Awareness-stage blog posts, 2 SEO-optimized landing pages, 1 gated framework comparison guide. Month 4–6: 2 Consideration-stage whitepapers, 1 interactive maturity assessment tool, 3 case studies. Month 7–12: ROI calculator, 2 webinars, 1 podcast series (CISO interviews), 6 additional blog posts. Total: 32 content assets over 12 months.

**Step 4 — Channel strategy:**
Primary channels: organic search (SEO targeting 24 high-intent keywords with monthly search volume 200–2,000), LinkedIn (CISO-targeted sponsored content and organic thought leadership from named consultants), email nurture (3 sequences: new subscriber, framework guide download, webinar registrant). Secondary channels: podcast guest appearances (3 security podcasts with CISO audiences), conference content (RSA, ISC2 local chapters). No paid search in months 1–6; introduce retargeting in month 7 based on content performance data.

**Step 5 — MQL scoring model definition:**
MQL threshold set at 45 points. Scoring criteria: job title match (CISO/VP Security = 20 pts, Director = 10 pts), company size 500–5,000 employees (15 pts), industry match financial services or healthcare (10 pts), content engagement: whitepaper download (10 pts), webinar attendance (15 pts), 3+ blog visits (5 pts), email open rate >40% over 4 weeks (5 pts). SQLs defined as MQLs with a scheduled discovery call request or RFP inquiry.

**Step 6 — Six-month targets and measurement framework:**
Month 6 targets: 500 net new email subscribers, 200 MQLs generated, 30 SQLs (15% MQL-to-SQL conversion), 5 new inbound opportunities in pipeline (avg ACV $180K = $900K pipeline). KPIs tracked weekly: organic sessions, MQL volume, MQL-to-SQL rate, pipeline created. Monthly review with leadership on content performance and channel ROI.
