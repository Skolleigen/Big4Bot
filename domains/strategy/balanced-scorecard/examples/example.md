# Balanced Scorecard — Example

## Scenario
A B2B software company providing compliance management tools has completed a strategy refresh and identified its winning aspiration as becoming the highest-retention platform in its category by expanding workflow automation capabilities and deepening customer success investment. The CEO has asked the strategy team to translate this direction into a balanced scorecard before the upcoming board meeting.

## Inputs
- **Organizational strategy:** Retain and expand existing enterprise accounts through workflow automation investment; deprioritize new logo acquisition in favor of net revenue retention above 115%
- **Financial data:** ARR $48M; NRR 102%; gross margin 71%; CAC payback 22 months; target NRR 115% in 24 months
- **Customer data:** NPS 34 (industry median 42); average customer health score 61/100; churn concentrated in accounts with <3 active users per seat license
- **Internal process data:** Median time-to-value (onboarding to first workflow live) 47 days; support ticket resolution SLA met 68% of the time; product release cycle 6 weeks
- **Learning and growth data:** Customer success manager (CSM) to account ratio 1:82 (industry benchmark 1:50); engineering team velocity declining 8% per quarter due to technical debt backlog

## Analysis

### Step 1 — Clarify Strategy
The strategy is retention-led growth through automation depth and customer success capacity. New logo targets are secondary. All scorecard objectives must be traceable to improving NRR and reducing churn in low-engagement accounts.

### Step 2 — Define Objectives per Perspective

**Financial:**
- Increase net revenue retention from 102% to 115%
- Improve gross margin from 71% to 74% through infrastructure efficiency

**Customer:**
- Improve NPS from 34 to 50 within 18 months
- Reduce churn in low-engagement accounts (fewer than 3 active users per license) by 40%

**Internal Processes:**
- Reduce time-to-value from 47 days to 21 days
- Increase SLA compliance for support ticket resolution from 68% to 90%
- Reduce technical debt backlog by 30% to restore engineering velocity

**Learning and Growth:**
- Improve CSM-to-account ratio from 1:82 to 1:55 through hiring and tooling
- Train all CSMs on new automation feature set within 60 days of release

### Step 3 — Identify KPIs
- Financial: NRR (monthly), gross margin (quarterly), expansion ARR per account (monthly)
- Customer: NPS (quarterly), logo churn rate (monthly), active user penetration rate per license (monthly)
- Internal Processes: Median days to first workflow live (monthly), SLA compliance rate (weekly), technical debt story point completion (sprint-level)
- Learning and Growth: CSM-to-account ratio (monthly), CSM certification completion rate (post-release), employee engagement score (semi-annual)

### Step 4 — Set Targets
All targets are set for a 24-month horizon with 12-month interim checkpoints. NRR target of 115% requires 8 percentage points of improvement, achievable if active user penetration increases from current 31% to 60% in churned-risk accounts. Time-to-value target of 21 days requires onboarding process redesign and CSM tool investment.

### Step 5 — Define Strategic Initiatives
- Initiative 1: Onboarding redesign program (targets: time-to-value, NPS) — Owner: VP Customer Success
- Initiative 2: CSM capacity expansion — hire 6 CSMs and deploy digital success tooling (targets: CSM ratio, churn rate) — Owner: Chief Customer Officer
- Initiative 3: Technical debt reduction sprint allocation — reserve 30% of engineering capacity per quarter (targets: velocity, SLA compliance) — Owner: CTO
- Initiative 4: Workflow automation accelerator module — three new no-code automation templates per quarter (targets: active user penetration, NRR) — Owner: VP Product

### Step 6 — Build Strategy Map
Learning and growth (CSM capacity, CSM skills) enables better internal processes (faster time-to-value, higher SLA compliance, more automation templates released) which drives customer outcomes (higher NPS, lower churn, deeper engagement) which produces financial results (higher NRR, improved gross margin through reduced churn-related cost). Causal links are directional and testable at each review cycle.

### Step 7 — Assign Ownership
Each objective has a named executive owner accountable at monthly executive review. Board reporting covers financial perspective quarterly; customer perspective reported monthly to CEO.
