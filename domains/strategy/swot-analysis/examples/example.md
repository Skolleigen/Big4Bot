# SWOT Analysis — Example

## Scenario
A mid-sized regional grocery chain with 42 stores is preparing its three-year strategic plan. The company has stable but declining same-store sales growth (-1.2% YoY) and faces intensifying competition from a national discount grocer that opened 8 locations in its core markets over the past 18 months. Leadership wants a structured diagnostic before committing capital to store remodels or a private-label expansion.

## Inputs
- **Internal performance data:** Same-store sales growth -1.2% YoY; gross margin 24.1% vs. industry median 22.8%; customer loyalty program covers 67% of transactions; supply chain cost per case $0.42 vs. industry benchmark $0.38
- **Organizational capability inventory:** Proprietary regional distribution network; 18-year average supplier relationship tenure; 340-person store management team with <8% annual turnover; no e-commerce fulfillment infrastructure
- **External intelligence:** Discount grocer entered 8 core markets; online grocery penetration in region reached 14% (up from 6% three years ago); SNAP benefit utilization in core demographic growing 9% annually; two regional competitors closed 11 stores combined in past 24 months
- **Stakeholder input:** Store manager surveys indicate inventory replenishment inconsistency as top operational complaint; customer exit surveys rank produce quality first among purchase drivers

## Analysis

### Step 1 — Establish Internal Performance Baseline
Financial metrics confirm margin advantage over the discount entrant (24.1% gross margin vs. estimated 19% for the discount competitor) but reveal supply chain unit cost gap of $0.04/case relative to industry benchmark. Loyalty program coverage at 67% of transactions provides a strong behavioral data asset.

### Step 2 — Identify Strengths
- Above-median gross margin driven by strong supplier relationships and private-label mix
- Proprietary regional distribution network enabling next-day replenishment to all 42 stores
- High loyalty program penetration generating transaction-level customer data
- Low store management turnover preserving institutional knowledge and customer relationships
- Produce quality rated highest purchase driver by customers — defensible differentiation

### Step 3 — Identify Weaknesses
- Supply chain cost per case $0.04 above industry benchmark, eroding price competitiveness at shelf
- No e-commerce fulfillment capability as online penetration reaches 14% of regional market
- Inventory replenishment inconsistency flagged by 78% of store managers — operational reliability gap
- Same-store sales declining despite margin advantage, indicating top-line demand erosion

### Step 4 — Identify Opportunities
- SNAP benefit growth (9% annually) represents addressable volume in value-tier categories
- Competitor store closures (11 stores) create whitespace for customer acquisition in 4 specific zip codes
- Online grocery penetration at 14% and rising — first-mover opportunity for regional omnichannel offering
- Private-label expansion leveraging existing supplier relationships could improve margins further and differentiate on value

### Step 5 — Identify Threats
- Discount grocer expansion trajectory suggests 6–10 additional locations likely within 24 months
- Online fulfillment gap risks accelerating customer attrition as digital-native competitors scale
- Commodity inflation pressure on produce and dairy could compress the gross margin advantage
- Regional economic softness may shift consumer preference toward discount formats structurally

### Step 6 — Cross-Quadrant Strategic Options
- **SO:** Use loyalty data and produce differentiation to launch targeted promotions capturing customers displaced by competitor store closures (Strength + Opportunity)
- **ST:** Accelerate private-label expansion to widen price gap against discount entrant using existing supplier network (Strength + Threat)
- **WO:** Invest in click-and-collect fulfillment infrastructure at high-volume stores to capture growing online demand before discount grocer does (Weakness + Opportunity)
- **WT:** Close supply chain cost gap through route optimization to protect margin if commodity inflation compresses gross margin (Weakness + Threat)

### Step 7 — Prioritize Options
Ranked by impact magnitude and execution feasibility within 18 months: (1) private-label expansion — high impact, low capital requirement; (2) click-and-collect at top 10 stores — high impact, moderate capital; (3) loyalty-driven win-back targeting competitor closure zip codes — moderate impact, low cost; (4) supply chain cost reduction program — high impact, 12–18 month timeline.

### Step 8 — Connect to Planning Artifacts
Each option is assigned to a strategic initiative owner and mapped to a balanced scorecard objective in the subsequent planning phase.
