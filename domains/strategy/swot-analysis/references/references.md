# SWOT Analysis — References

## Foundational Works
- Albert Humphrey (SRI International): *SWOT Framework* (1960s) — Originated during a Stanford Research Institute project studying why Fortune 500 companies failed to implement long-range plans; introduced the four-quadrant internal/external diagnostic structure
- Heinz Weihrich: *The TOWS Matrix — A Tool for Situational Analysis* (1982) — Extended the SWOT inventory into an actionable cross-impact matrix generating SO, ST, WO, and WT strategic option categories; published in Long Range Planning journal

## Academic Sources
- George Steiner: *Strategic Planning* (1979) — Provided early academic grounding for environmental scanning and internal appraisal as preconditions to strategy formulation, contextualizing SWOT within formal planning processes
- Kenneth Andrews: *The Concept of Corporate Strategy* (1971) — Introduced the LCAG (Learned, Christensen, Andrews, Guth) framework distinguishing organizational competences from environmental conditions, the conceptual predecessor to SWOT

## Practitioner Resources
- Michael E. Porter: *Competitive Strategy* (1980) — Porter's Five Forces model (industry rivalry, buyer power, supplier power, threat of substitutes, threat of new entrants) provides a structured external scanning methodology complementary to the SWOT opportunity and threat quadrants
- McKinsey & Company: *Strategic Planning Frameworks* — Practitioner guidance on integrating SWOT outputs into downstream strategy formulation artifacts including initiative prioritization and strategic roadmaps
- Harvard Business Review: *Bringing Science to the Art of Strategy* (2012, Martin, Lafley) — Critiques unstructured SWOT usage and establishes conditions under which the framework produces actionable versus decorative outputs
