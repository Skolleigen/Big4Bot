# Visioning and Reverse Engineering — Example

## Scenario
The CEO of a $300M regional healthcare services company wants to reposition the business as a national care coordination platform within 7 years. The current state is a regionally concentrated business: 3 states, 12 clinics, $300M revenue, 1,800 employees, with a largely paper-based coordination model. The board has endorsed an ambitious growth mandate but leadership requires a structured process to translate the ambition into a credible, reverse-engineered strategic roadmap.

## Inputs
- **Planning horizon:** 7 years (confirmed by board resolution)
- **Current state baseline:** $300M revenue, 3 states, 12 clinic sites, 1,800 employees, 4 payer contracts, paper/phone-based care coordination
- **Future state ambition:** $1.2B revenue, national presence across 15 states, 85+ sites, technology-enabled care coordination platform
- **Stakeholder ambition inputs:** Board expects 4x revenue growth; CEO targets top-3 national market position in care coordination by Year 7
- **Market context:** Care coordination market growing at 11% CAGR; three well-capitalized competitors already at national scale

## Analysis

**Step 1 — Visioning session:**
A two-day leadership offsite produces a concrete future state definition. Year 7 target: $1.2B revenue, 15-state footprint, 85+ sites (mix of owned and affiliated), proprietary care coordination technology platform serving 250,000 active patients, and a nationally recognized employer brand enabling clinical talent recruitment at scale. The future state is documented with quantitative anchors for each dimension.

**Step 2 — Vision stress-testing:**
The $1.2B target implies a 22% CAGR — significantly above the 11% market rate, requiring market share gain. Stress-testing confirms this is achievable only if M&A contributes approximately 40% of growth (organic alone produces $680M by Year 7). The technology platform is confirmed as structurally necessary: at 85 sites, paper-based coordination creates unacceptable quality risk and regulatory exposure. Competitive dynamics analysis flags that two of the three national competitors are currently acquisition-active, creating a 3-year window for geographic consolidation before prime targets are absorbed.

**Step 3 — Future state documentation:**
Future state mapped across four dimensions: Financial (revenue $1.2B, EBITDA margin 18%, capital structure supporting M&A); Market Position (national care coordination brand, 15-state coverage, 3 specialty service lines); Capabilities (proprietary technology platform, M&A integration playbook, clinical governance at scale, regulatory affairs function); Culture (performance-driven, clinically excellent, mission-aligned employer of choice).

**Step 4 — Gap mapping:**
Current vs. future state gaps quantified: Revenue gap $900M; geographic gap 12 additional states; site gap 73 additional sites; technology gap from paper-based to a proprietary platform (currently non-existent); M&A capability gap (no dedicated M&A team or integration playbook); regulatory affairs gap (currently 4 payer contracts; national scale requires 60+). The technology and M&A capability gaps are identified as the longest lead-time items.

**Step 5 — Strategic theme decomposition:**
Four strategic themes identified as the organizing architecture: (1) Technology Platform — build or acquire proprietary care coordination technology; (2) M&A and Geographic Expansion — structured acquisition strategy targeting regional consolidators in identified states; (3) Clinical Talent and Employer Brand — create national recruiting and retention infrastructure; (4) Regulatory and Payer Relationships — build payer contracting function capable of national scale.

**Step 6 — Milestone reverse engineering:**
Working backward from Year 7: Year 7 — $1.2B revenue, 15 states, platform fully operational. Year 5 — $750M revenue, 10 states, platform live in 8 states, M&A integration playbook proven across 4 acquisitions. Year 3 — $480M revenue, 7 states, technology platform in beta across 3 legacy sites, first 2 acquisitions closed and integrated. Year 1 — $335M revenue (organic growth only), technology vendor selected and implementation begun, M&A team of 4 hired, first acquisition letter of intent signed.

**Step 7 — Initiative and accountability mapping:**
Year 1 milestones decomposed into 9 initiatives with leadership accountability. Technology platform selection assigned to Chief Digital Officer (new hire required by Q2). M&A capability build assigned to CFO with target of engaging a dedicated M&A director by Month 4. Clinical talent strategy assigned to CHRO. Each milestone is entered into the strategy scorecard as a leading indicator of Year 7 trajectory.
