# Visioning and Reverse Engineering — References

## Foundational Works
- Jim Collins & Jerry Porras: *Built to Last: Successful Habits of Visionary Companies* (1994) — Introduced the Big Hairy Audacious Goal (BHAG) concept as a long-horizon visioning anchor; demonstrated through longitudinal research that visionary companies consistently define concrete 10–30 year future state targets that challenge incrementalism
- Gary Hamel & C.K. Prahalad: *Strategic Intent*, Harvard Business Review (1989) — Established the concept of strategic intent as a future competitive position that the organization obsessively pursues; distinguished intent-driven strategy from resource-bounded incremental planning and introduced the reverse engineering logic of working backward from destination to capability requirements

## Academic Sources
- Richard Rumelt: *Good Strategy Bad Strategy: The Difference and Why It Matters* (2011) — Critiques vision statements that lack a concrete diagnosis of the gap between current and desired state; provides analytical grounding for the gap decomposition step in reverse engineering
- C.K. Prahalad & Gary Hamel: *The Core Competence of the Corporation*, Harvard Business Review (1990) — Establishes that future state achievement requires explicit identification and investment in the core competencies the organization currently lacks; foundational for the capability gap mapping step

## Practitioner Resources
- Roger Martin & A.G. Lafley: *Playing to Win: How Strategy Really Works* (2013) — Provides the "where to play / how to win" framework that structures the concrete operational dimensions of a future state vision; extensively used in reverse engineering to define future state with precision rather than aspiration alone
- McKinsey & Company: *The alchemy of growth* (Baghai, Coley & White, 2000) — Three Horizons framework for organizing initiatives by time horizon aligns directly with milestone sequencing in reverse engineering; provides a practitioner tool for categorizing the reverse-engineered initiative map
- Bain & Company: *Backcasting: Strategic Planning from the Future* — Practitioner methodology guide for facilitating leadership visioning and milestone reverse engineering; includes facilitation design and stress-testing approaches for future state validation
