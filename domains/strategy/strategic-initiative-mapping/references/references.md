# Strategic Initiative Mapping — References

## Foundational Works
- Robert S. Kaplan & David P. Norton: *Strategy Maps: Converting Intangible Assets into Tangible Outcomes* (2004) — Extends the Balanced Scorecard by providing a visual framework for linking strategic initiatives to cause-and-effect chains across four perspectives; foundational for connecting initiative selection to strategic objective architecture
- Roger Martin & A.G. Lafley: *Playing to Win: How Strategy Really Works* (2013) — Articulates the "winning aspiration and where-to-play/how-to-win" logic that defines which strategic priorities initiatives should serve; provides the decision criteria underlying initiative selection

## Academic Sources
- Michael E. Porter: *What Is Strategy?*, Harvard Business Review (1996) — Distinguishes operational effectiveness from strategic positioning; establishes that initiative selection must reinforce a distinctive strategic configuration rather than pursue isolated improvements
- C.K. Prahalad & Gary Hamel: *The Core Competence of the Corporation*, Harvard Business Review (1990) — Argues that strategic initiatives should build and leverage core competencies; provides a competence-based filter for initiative evaluation alongside financial feasibility

## Practitioner Resources
- McKinsey & Company: *Strategic Initiative Prioritization* — Practitioner guidance on portfolio selection methodologies including impact-feasibility matrix construction and resource envelope application across initiative portfolios
- Project Management Institute: *The Standard for Portfolio Management*, 4th Edition (2017) — Defines portfolio component selection, categorization and prioritization processes; provides governance standards for initiative portfolio decisions
- Bain & Company: *Management Tools — Strategic Planning* — Annual practitioner survey and guidance on initiative prioritization tools used by senior executives across industries and geographies
