# Strategic Initiative Mapping

## Domain
Strategy

## Description
Structured methodology for identifying, prioritizing and sequencing strategic initiatives that close the gap between current state and strategic objectives. Produces a phased initiative roadmap with explicit resource allocation and accountability assignments.

## Purpose
Organizations generate many initiative candidates during strategy cycles but rarely have a systematic method for selecting, sequencing and resourcing them. This framework evaluates each initiative against strategic objectives, feasibility constraints and inter-initiative dependencies to determine which initiatives belong in the active portfolio, which should be held in reserve, and in what order execution should proceed. The output creates a defensible, resource-balanced initiative roadmap that connects strategic intent to execution planning.

## When to Use
- Following a strategy refresh when execution priorities must be defined and communicated
- When the initiative backlog is unstructured and lacks transparent prioritization criteria
- When resource constraints (budget, headcount, time) require explicit trade-off decisions across candidate initiatives
- When dependencies between initiatives create sequencing requirements that affect feasibility

## When NOT to Use
- When a single high-priority initiative is already defined, approved and in execution
- When the organization lacks a confirmed strategy to map initiatives against
- When the organization is in early exploratory mode with no concrete initiative candidates yet identified

## Inputs Required
- Strategic objectives with priority weighting (must-win battles, growth pillars, or equivalent)
- Full list of candidate initiatives with rough scoping, sponsor and strategic theme classification
- Resource envelope defining available budget, headcount and time horizon
- Existing commitments, run-the-business obligations and known dependencies between initiatives

## Optional Inputs
- Initiative feasibility assessments or pre-read business cases
- Sponsor alignment status and political readiness signals
- External benchmarks on implementation timelines for comparable initiatives

## Diagnostic Process
1. Review confirmed strategic objectives and their relative priority weighting to establish the scoring anchor
2. Compile the full list of candidate initiatives from all sources — strategy sessions, business units, functions — and eliminate duplicates
3. Score each initiative on two dimensions: strategic impact (degree of alignment to weighted objectives) and feasibility (cost, complexity, required time and capability availability)
4. Plot initiatives on a 2x2 impact-feasibility matrix and identify the quadrant distribution — high impact/high feasibility initiatives are primary candidates; high impact/low feasibility require phasing or enablers
5. Identify dependencies between initiatives (enabling, conflicting or sequencing constraints) and annotate the matrix accordingly
6. Apply the resource envelope to select the active portfolio, sequence initiatives into phases (now, near, later), and designate reserve initiatives
7. Assign initiative sponsors, delivery owners and success metrics to each active initiative and document the portfolio roadmap with rationale for inclusion and exclusion decisions

## Output
The output is a structured initiative roadmap mapping each selected initiative to one or more strategic objectives, a delivery phase and resource assignment, accompanied by a reserve list with conditions for activation. Supporting documentation captures the scoring rationale, dependency map and resource allocation summary. Together these artifacts give leadership a defensible, transparent basis for execution planning and resource governance.
