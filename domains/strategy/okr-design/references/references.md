# OKR Design — References

## Foundational Works
- Andy Grove: *High Output Management* (Random House, 1983) — Established Management by Objectives (MBO) as a production management discipline at Intel, introducing the principle that managers should be measured by the output of their organizations; the direct intellectual predecessor to OKRs
- John Doerr: *Measure What Matters: How Google, Bono, and the Gates Foundation Rock the World with OKRs* (Portfolio/Penguin, 2018) — Primary practitioner text documenting OKR methodology, implementation, and case studies; introduced the CFR (Conversations, Feedback, Recognition) complement to OKRs

## Academic Sources
- Edwin Locke and Gary Latham: *A Theory of Goal Setting and Task Performance* (Prentice Hall, 1990) — Foundational academic research establishing that specific, challenging goals consistently produce higher performance than vague or easy goals; provides the empirical basis for OKR stretch targets
- Gary Latham and Edwin Locke: *Building a Practically Useful Theory of Goal Setting and Task Motivation* (American Psychologist, 2002) — Meta-analysis of 35 years of goal-setting research confirming the conditions under which specific ambitious goals improve performance

## Practitioner Resources
- Google re:Work: *Set Goals with OKRs* — Google's public OKR implementation guide covering the 0.0–1.0 scoring convention, the 70% expected attainment norm for aspirational OKRs, and the distinction between committed and aspirational goal types
- Felipe Castro: *The OKR Cycle* — Practitioner framework for the quarterly OKR design, tracking, and retrospective cadence, including anti-patterns observed across OKR implementations at scale
- Christina Wodtke: *Radical Focus: Achieving Your Most Important Goals with Objectives and Key Results* (Cucina Media, 2016) — Narrative-format practitioner guide emphasizing the importance of focus and the failure modes of setting too many OKRs simultaneously
