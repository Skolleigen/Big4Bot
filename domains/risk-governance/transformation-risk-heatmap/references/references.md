# Transformation Risk Heatmap — References

## Foundational Works
- PMI (Project Management Institute): *Managing Change in Organizations: A Practice Guide* (2013) — The foundational PMI reference for transformation risk management; integrates change management disciplines with project risk frameworks and provides guidance on risk identification across people, process, and technology dimensions of organizational change
- John Kotter: *Leading Change* (1996) — Seminal work on transformation leadership; Kotter's 8-step model identifies the most common transformation failure modes, which directly inform transformation risk taxonomy construction including urgency, coalition, vision, and sustaining momentum risks
- Daryl Conner: *Managing at the Speed of Change* (1992) — Practitioner-academic framework for organizational change resilience; introduces the concept of change saturation and change capacity — the theoretical foundation for "change fatigue" as a transformation risk category

## Academic Sources
- McKinsey Global Institute: *The State of Organizations* and *Unlocking Success in Digital Transformations* (2018) — Quantitative research across 800 digital transformation programs; documents that 70% of transformations fail to meet their goals and identifies the top risk factors including talent, agile adoption, and capability building; widely cited in transformation risk literature
- Jeanne Ross, Cynthia Beath & Martin Mocker: *Designed for Digital: How to Architect Your Business for Sustained Success* (2019) — Academic-practitioner research on digital transformation risk factors; emphasizes integration complexity and legacy system entanglement as the primary technical risk in digital transformations
- Sonja Sackmann, Marc Eggenhofer-Rehart & Martina Friesl: *Sustainable Change: Long-Term Efforts toward Developing a Learning Organization*, Journal of Applied Behavioral Science (2009) — Academic research on change sustainability and the organizational conditions that determine whether transformation benefits are realized; relevant to post-go-live risk management
- Torben Beck Jorgensen & Barry Bozeman: *Public Values: An Inventory*, Administration & Society (2007) — Research on stakeholder value conflicts in large-scale transformation programs; useful for understanding resistance risk in multi-stakeholder environments

## Practitioner Resources
- McKinsey & Company: *Transformation with a Capital T* and *The Transformation Imperative* (McKinsey Quarterly) — Practitioner perspective on the risk factors specific to large-scale organizational transformations; includes frameworks for risk prioritization and governance design in multi-year programs
- KPMG: *Major Programme Management Methodology* and *Transformation Risk Framework* — Leading Big-4 practitioner methodology for major program risk management; includes a pre-built transformation risk taxonomy, heatmap visualization standards, and steering governance design for multi-year programs
- Prosci: *ADKAR Model and Change Risk Assessment Framework* — The leading practitioner change management methodology; the Prosci Change Risk Assessment tool quantifies people and adoption risks that are the most underweighted category in traditional technology-focused program risk registers
- Bent Flyvbjerg & Dan Gardner: *How Big Things Get Done* (2023) — Data-driven analysis of major project and transformation risk patterns across thousands of projects; documents systematic optimism bias in transformation plans and provides empirical risk benchmarks for schedule, cost, and scope outcomes
