# Risk Register — References

## Foundational Works
- ISO 31000:2018: *Risk Management — Guidelines* — The international standard for risk management principles, framework, and process; provides the foundational vocabulary, risk assessment methodology, and risk treatment guidance upon which enterprise risk registers are built; replaces and supersedes ISO 31000:2009
- COSO: *Enterprise Risk Management — Integrating with Strategy and Performance* (2017) — The updated COSO ERM Framework integrating risk management with strategic planning and performance management; defines risk appetite, risk tolerance, and risk response categories used in risk register construction
- PMI (Project Management Institute): *A Guide to the Project Management Body of Knowledge (PMBOK Guide)*, 7th Edition — The standard reference for project risk management; includes detailed guidance on risk identification, qualitative analysis (probability-impact matrix), quantitative analysis, and risk response planning

## Academic Sources
- David Hillson: *Effective Opportunity Management for Projects: Exploiting Positive Risk* (2004) — Extends traditional risk register methodology to include opportunity identification; provides academic grounding for treating risk as both threat and opportunity in project contexts
- Karen Bogen & Elizabeth Longstaff: *Toward a New Understanding of Risk*, CERT Coordination Center, Carnegie Mellon University (1994) — Foundational academic paper on risk taxonomy and the challenge of risk categorization in complex organizational systems
- Wim Van Grembergen & Steven De Haes: *Enterprise Governance of Information Technology*, Springer (2009) — Academic framework linking IT risk registers to enterprise governance structures; relevant to technology-heavy implementations like ERP programs
- Nik Aakvik: *Risk Management Revisited: A Conceptual Framework*, International Journal of Project Management (2001) — Peer-reviewed examination of risk identification techniques and the validity of different risk elicitation methods (workshops, interviews, historical analysis)

## Practitioner Resources
- PMI: *Practice Standard for Project Risk Management* (2009) — Detailed practitioner guidance on risk register construction, maintenance, and integration with project governance; includes templates, example risk descriptions, and escalation criteria
- PRINCE2 (OGC/Axelos): *Managing Successful Projects with PRINCE2* — Widely used project management methodology with a comprehensive risk management procedure including risk register format, risk appetite documentation, and risk tolerance thresholds
- IRM (Institute of Risk Management): *A Risk Management Standard* — UK-based professional body standard covering risk register design, risk scoring approaches, and organizational risk governance structures
- Deloitte / PwC ERP Risk Frameworks: *ERP Implementation Risk Assessment Methodology* — Practitioner risk taxonomies specifically designed for large ERP implementations (SAP, Oracle, Workday); includes pre-seeded risk libraries for data migration, change management, integration, and go-live cutover risk categories
