# Capability Maturity Assessment — References

## Foundational Works
- CMMI Institute: *CMMI for Development, Version 2.0* (2018) — The authoritative reference for the Capability Maturity Model Integration framework; defines the five maturity levels, process areas, and specific practices used as the basis for capability assessment across engineering and management domains
- Software Engineering Institute (SEI), Carnegie Mellon University: *The Capability Maturity Model: Guidelines for Improving the Software Process* (1995) — Original academic foundation for CMM; established the five-level maturity progression concept that has been adapted for risk, governance, and organizational capability assessment
- OGC (Office of Government Commerce): *P3M3 — Portfolio, Programme and Project Management Maturity Model* (2010) — Alternative maturity model widely used in UK public sector and large program environments; provides a complementary perspective on organizational capability assessment with a focus on delivery management

## Academic Sources
- Watts S. Humphrey: *Managing the Software Process* (1989) — The original academic work introducing process maturity concepts that became the foundation for CMM; establishes the theoretical basis for using maturity levels as a diagnostic and improvement framework
- Mark C. Paulk, Bill Curtis, Mary Beth Chrissis & Charles V. Weber: *Capability Maturity Model for Software, Version 1.1*, SEI Technical Report CMU/SEI-93-TR-024 (1993) — The seminal academic paper defining CMM maturity levels and key process areas; the most widely cited source in capability assessment literature
- Richard Klimoski & Susan Mohammed: *Team Mental Model: Construct or Metaphor?*, Journal of Management (1994) — Research on shared mental models relevant to capability assessment's reliance on multi-stakeholder interview triangulation for accurate scoring
- Nils Fonstad & David Robertson: *Transforming a Company, Project by Project: The IT Engagement Model*, MIS Quarterly Executive (2006) — Academic research on IT governance maturity and its relationship to organizational performance outcomes

## Practitioner Resources
- Gartner: *IT Score for Infrastructure and Operations Maturity Model* — Widely used practitioner framework for assessing IT capability maturity; includes benchmarking data against industry peers and prescriptive improvement guidance for each maturity level
- ISACA: *COBIT 2019 Capability and Maturity Assessment Guidance* — Governance-focused capability assessment framework aligned to COBIT 2019; provides detailed assessment criteria for IT and information governance domains commonly evaluated in regulatory contexts
- Deloitte: *Capability Maturity Assessment Methodology for Financial Services* — Practitioner methodology adapted for regulatory examination preparation in banking and insurance; includes OCC, Fed, and FINRA examination alignment guidance
- APQC (American Productivity & Quality Center): *Process and Performance Management Benchmarks* — Cross-industry benchmarking database for process maturity; provides peer comparison data to contextualize maturity assessment scores against industry norms
