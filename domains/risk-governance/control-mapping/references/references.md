# Control Mapping — References

## Foundational Works
- Committee of Sponsoring Organizations of the Treadway Commission (COSO): *Internal Control — Integrated Framework* (2013) — The globally recognized standard for internal control design and assessment; defines the five components (Control Environment, Risk Assessment, Control Activities, Information & Communication, Monitoring) and 17 principles that form the foundation of all control mapping work
- AICPA: *SOC 2 Trust Service Criteria* (2017, updated 2022) — Authoritative source for SOC 2 control requirements; defines the Common Criteria (CC), Availability (A), Confidentiality (C), Processing Integrity (PI), and Privacy (P) trust service criteria against which controls are mapped and tested
- ISACA: *COBIT 2019 Framework: Governance and Management Objectives* — Comprehensive IT governance framework providing a detailed control objective library that maps to multiple compliance frameworks including SOC 2, ISO 27001, and NIST CSF; widely used as a cross-walk source in multi-framework control mapping

## Academic Sources
- Lawrence Gordon & Martin Loeb: *The Economics of Information Security Investment*, ACM Transactions on Information and System Security (2002) — Foundational academic paper establishing the economic framework for control investment decisions; relevant to prioritizing gap remediation by expected loss reduction
- Paul Doocey & Noel Carroll: *IT Governance and Business Process Compliance*, Business Information Review (2013) — Academic examination of how control mapping frameworks integrate with business process management; addresses the organizational challenges of cross-domain control ownership
- Steven Snyder & Robert Blanning: *A Framework for IT Control Risk Assessment*, Journal of Information Systems (2014) — Peer-reviewed methodology for assessing control design adequacy and operating effectiveness; provides academic grounding for the two-stage assessment approach (design then effectiveness)
- Mikko Siponen & Adam Mahmood: *Toward a Unified Model of Information Security Policy Compliance*, MIS Quarterly (2010) — Research on factors affecting control operating effectiveness; relevant to understanding why adequately-designed controls fail to operate consistently

## Practitioner Resources
- AICPA: *Guide to the Use of the AICPA's SOC 2 Examination Report* — Practitioner guidance on SOC 2 scoping, control selection, and evidence requirements; essential reference for mapping controls to the trust service criteria and understanding what constitutes sufficient operating effectiveness evidence
- IIA (Institute of Internal Auditors): *International Standards for the Professional Practice of Internal Auditing* — Professional standards governing control assessment methodology; provides guidance on testing approaches, sampling requirements, and evidence documentation for internal and external auditors
- NIST: *NIST SP 800-53 Security and Privacy Controls for Information Systems and Organizations* (Rev. 5, 2020) — The most comprehensive control catalog available; widely used as a source library in multi-framework mapping projects; each control includes implementation guidance and control enhancements
- Schellman & Company / Coalfire: *SOC 2 Readiness Assessment Methodology* — Leading practitioner methodology published by major SOC 2 audit firms; provides practical guidance on control gap identification, evidence repository structure, and pre-audit remediation prioritization
