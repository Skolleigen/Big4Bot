# References

- **Bruner, Robert F. (2004).** *Applied Mergers and Acquisitions*. John Wiley & Sons.
  - A comprehensive academic and practitioner guide bridging the gap between theoretical valuation and practical due diligence. It outlines how strategic, financial, and operational diligence loops back to adjust the valuation framework.

- **McKinsey & Company (Koller, Goedhart, & Wessels, 2020).** *Valuation: Measuring and Managing the Value of Companies* (7th ed.). John Wiley & Sons.
  - Particularly the sections concerning M&A, this text emphasizes that a DCF is only as good as the diligence validating its inputs, strongly advising rigorous synergy validation and Quality of Earnings (QoE) scrutiny to avoid "winner's curse" overpayment.

- **Harding, D., & Rovit, S. (2004).** *Mastering the Merger: Four Critical Decisions That Make or Break the Deal*. Harvard Business Review Press.
  - This book shifted the M&A paradigm from accounting-focused diligence to "thesis-driven" diligence, arguing that diligence must prioritize proving or disproving the core strategic rationale for the deal (e.g., cross-selling, capability acquisition).
