# Comparable Company Valuation — References

## Foundational Works
- Aswath Damodaran: *Investment Valuation: Tools and Techniques for Determining the Value of Any Asset* (2002) — The authoritative academic reference on equity valuation, with dedicated chapters on relative valuation, multiples selection, and the limitations of comparable company analysis.
- Tim Koller, Marc Goedhart, David Wessels (McKinsey & Company): *Valuation: Measuring and Managing the Value of Companies* (2020, 7th ed.) — The leading practitioner-academic hybrid reference for enterprise valuation, covering comparable company methodology, multiple calibration, and private company adjustments.
- Shannon P. Pratt: *Valuing a Business: The Analysis and Appraisal of Closely Held Companies* (2008) — Definitive reference for applying public market multiples to private company valuation, including illiquidity and minority interest discount frameworks.

## Academic Sources
- CFA Institute: *CFA Program Curriculum, Level 2: Equity Valuation* — Comprehensive treatment of price and enterprise value multiples, including EV/EBITDA, EV/Revenue, P/E, and their theoretical underpinnings and practical application in comparable analysis.
- Aswath Damodaran: *The Dark Side of Valuation* (2009) — Examines limitations of comparable company analysis, including selection bias in peer sets, circular reasoning in multiple-based valuations, and adjustments for non-comparable characteristics.
- Pavel Savor, Qi Lu: *Do Stock Mergers Create Value for Acquirers?*, Journal of Finance — Empirical evidence on how acquirers use comparable transaction multiples and the valuation errors arising from peer set selection.

## Practitioner Resources
- PitchBook / PitchBook Data: *Private Company Valuation Reports* — Industry-leading database of private company valuations, comparable transaction multiples, and VC/PE deal pricing by sector and stage.
- Goldman Sachs Equity Research: *SaaS and Cloud Software Valuation Framework* — Practitioner framework used by institutional investors to value high-growth SaaS companies, covering EV/Revenue, EV/ARR, and Rule of 40 correlation with multiples.
- Duff & Phelps (Kroll): *Cost of Capital Navigator and Valuation Handbook* — Annual publication providing market-based equity risk premia, size premia, and private company discount benchmarks used in comparable company valuation adjustments.
- Morgan Stanley / JPMorgan Equity Research: *Technology Sector Comparable Company Analyses* — Practitioner comps sheets illustrating how bulge-bracket banks screen and present comparable company multiples in M&A pitchbooks.
