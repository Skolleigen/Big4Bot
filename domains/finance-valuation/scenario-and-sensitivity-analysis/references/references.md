# Scenario & Sensitivity Analysis — References

## Foundational Works
- Aswath Damodaran: *Investment Valuation: Tools and Techniques for Determining the Value of Any Asset* (2012, 3rd ed.) — Comprehensive coverage of sensitivity and scenario analysis as components of DCF valuation, including Monte Carlo simulation, tornado diagrams, and break-even analysis in the context of investment decisions.
- Tim Koller, Marc Goedhart, David Wessels (McKinsey & Company): *Valuation: Measuring and Managing the Value of Companies* (2020, 7th ed.) — The McKinsey practitioner framework for scenario analysis in corporate valuation, including guidance on probability-weighted scenario construction and key value driver identification.
- Richard A. Brealey, Stewart C. Myers, Franklin Allen: *Principles of Corporate Finance* (2022, 14th ed.) — Foundational treatment of sensitivity analysis (NPV break-even, scenario testing) as part of capital budgeting decision-making under uncertainty.

## Academic Sources
- CFA Institute: *CFA Program Curriculum, Level 2: Equity Valuation and Capital Budgeting* — Covers sensitivity analysis, scenario analysis, and Monte Carlo simulation as standard components of equity valuation and project evaluation methodology.
- Hertz, David B.: *Risk Analysis in Capital Investment*, Harvard Business Review (1964) — Landmark article introducing Monte Carlo simulation to corporate capital budgeting, establishing the intellectual foundation for probabilistic sensitivity analysis.
- James E. Smith, Robert F. Nau: *Valuing Risky Projects: Option Pricing Theory and Decision Analysis*, Management Science (1995) — Academic treatment of scenario-based project valuation and the relationship between real options and traditional sensitivity analysis.

## Practitioner Resources
- McKinsey & Company: *Financial Modeling Best Practices* (internal standards, widely referenced in M&A advisory) — Defines the McKinsey approach to building integrated scenario models with clearly labeled bear, base, and bull cases linked to specific macro and company-level assumptions.
- Morgan Stanley / Goldman Sachs Equity Research: *Sensitivity Tables and Scenario Modeling in Equity Research* — Industry standard for presenting two-way sensitivity tables (e.g., EV vs. WACC and terminal growth rate) and scenario analyses in investment research reports.
- @RISK (Palisade) / Oracle Crystal Ball: *Monte Carlo Simulation for Financial Modeling* (Software Documentation) — Leading practitioner tools for implementing probabilistic sensitivity analysis with defined probability distributions on key input variables.
- Harvard Business School: *Note on Scenario Analysis in Strategic Planning* (Technical Note) — MBA pedagogical reference applying scenario analysis to strategic capital allocation decisions, including the use of tornado charts to rank driver importance.
