# Break-Even Analysis — References

## Foundational Works
- Charles T. Horngren, Srikant M. Datar, Madhav V. Rajan: *Cost Accounting: A Managerial Emphasis* (2021) — The definitive graduate-level cost accounting textbook covering CVP analysis, contribution margin, and break-even methodology in depth.
- Ray H. Garrison, Eric W. Noreen, Peter C. Brewer: *Managerial Accounting* (2021) — Widely adopted managerial accounting text with comprehensive treatment of cost-volume-profit analysis and break-even applications across industries.
- Charles T. Horngren, George Foster, Srikant M. Datar: *Cost Accounting: A Managerial Emphasis* (1994) — Earlier foundational edition that established the standard pedagogical framework for contribution margin and break-even analysis still used today.

## Academic Sources
- Robert S. Kaplan, Anthony A. Atkinson: *Advanced Management Accounting*, Prentice Hall — Graduate-level treatment of CVP analysis with multi-product break-even models and activity-based costing extensions.
- Eunsup Daniel Shim, Ephraim P. Smith: *The Effect of Inflation on the Break-Even Point*, Journal of Business Finance & Accounting — Examines how input cost inflation shifts break-even thresholds, directly relevant to variable cost sensitivity analysis.
- Michael W. Maher, Edward B. Deakin: *Cost Accounting*, Irwin McGraw-Hill — Covers margin of safety, operating leverage, and the relationship between break-even structure and business risk.

## Practitioner Resources
- McKinsey & Company: *Corporate Finance: Theory and Practice* — Practitioner framework connecting break-even analysis to operating leverage and business model scalability assessments.
- Andreessen Horowitz (a16z): *SaaS Metrics That Matter* — Applies break-even concepts to SaaS subscription businesses, including contribution margin benchmarks and fixed vs. variable cost structures.
- Harvard Business School Publishing: *Note on Breakeven Analysis* (Technical Note) — Concise pedagogical note used in MBA programs to apply break-even logic to real business investment decisions.
- CFO Magazine / CFO Research: *Working Capital and Cost Structure Benchmarks* — Annual benchmarking data useful for calibrating fixed and variable cost assumptions in break-even models by industry.
