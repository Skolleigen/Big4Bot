# DCF Valuation — References

## Foundational Works
- Aswath Damodaran: *Investment Valuation: Tools and Techniques for Determining the Value of Any Asset* (2002, 3rd ed. 2012) — The most comprehensive academic reference on DCF methodology, covering FCFF vs. FCFE models, WACC estimation, terminal value approaches, and adjustments for risk and growth.
- Tim Koller, Marc Goedhart, David Wessels (McKinsey & Company): *Valuation: Measuring and Managing the Value of Companies* (2020, 7th ed.) — The leading practitioner-academic hybrid reference, providing the McKinsey DCF framework used across investment banking and corporate finance globally.
- Richard A. Brealey, Stewart C. Myers, Franklin Allen: *Principles of Corporate Finance* (2022, 14th ed.) — Foundational corporate finance textbook with rigorous treatment of present value theory, cost of capital, and capital budgeting that underpins all DCF methodology.

## Academic Sources
- Aswath Damodaran: *The Little Book of Valuation* (2011) — Accessible practitioner companion to Investment Valuation, distilling DCF methodology into a practical framework for valuing growth companies including SaaS and software businesses.
- John Burr Williams: *The Theory of Investment Value* (1938) — The original academic work introducing dividend discount and present value frameworks that form the theoretical foundation of all discounted cash flow valuation.
- Stewart C. Myers: *Interactions of Corporate Financing and Investment Decisions — Implications for Capital Budgeting*, Journal of Finance (1974) — Foundational paper establishing the relationship between financing decisions, WACC, and enterprise value in DCF models.
- Eugene Fama, Kenneth French: *The Cross-Section of Expected Stock Returns*, Journal of Finance (1992) — The academic basis for the Fama-French model used as an alternative to CAPM in estimating the cost of equity for DCF discount rates.

## Practitioner Resources
- Duff & Phelps (Kroll): *Cost of Capital Navigator and Valuation Handbook* — The industry standard annual publication for market-based equity risk premia, size premia, and industry betas used to calibrate DCF discount rates.
- Goldman Sachs Investment Banking: *Financial Modeling and Valuation Techniques* (internal training materials, widely referenced) — Practitioner DCF framework used in bulge-bracket investment banking, covering integrated three-statement modeling and FCFF derivation.
- Damodaran Online (pages.stern.nyu.edu/~adamodar): *Datasets, Valuation Tools, and DCF Spreadsheets* — Publicly available annual datasets for equity risk premia, sector betas, WACC estimates, and growth rates by industry, maintained by Aswath Damodaran at NYU Stern.
- CFA Institute: *CFA Program Curriculum, Level 2: Equity Valuation* — Comprehensive treatment of multi-stage DDM and FCFF/FCFE DCF models, including terminal value methodologies and sensitivity analysis frameworks.
