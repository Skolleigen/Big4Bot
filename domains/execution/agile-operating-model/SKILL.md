# Agile Operating Model

## Domain
Execution

## Description
Organizational design framework that restructures delivery around cross-functional, autonomous teams operating in iterative cycles, enabling faster response to change. It replaces hierarchical, function-based structures with value-stream-oriented squads and tribes aligned to product or customer outcomes.

## Purpose
The agile operating model enables organizations to reduce time-to-market, improve quality, and increase responsiveness to customer and market signals. It realigns incentives, decision rights, and team topology so that authority sits closest to the work. Adopted correctly, it transforms both the delivery mechanism and the organizational culture that sustains it.

## When to Use
- The organization is experiencing slow delivery cycles or persistent backlogs caused by functional silos and sequential handoffs
- Leadership has committed to a product-led or digital transformation requiring structural and governance change
- Current planning processes cannot accommodate iterative or continuous delivery and need to be redesigned
- The organization needs to scale agile practices from individual teams to a department or enterprise level

## When NOT to Use
- The organization lacks executive sponsorship or has not completed a change-readiness assessment
- Teams operate in highly regulated environments where iterative delivery conflicts with compliance mandates and no mitigation path has been identified
- The engagement is scoped only to assess current-state delivery without authorization to recommend structural changes

## Inputs Required
- Current organizational chart and reporting structure, including spans of control
- Inventory of active delivery initiatives and programs with ownership mapping
- Product or service portfolio with identification of customer-facing value streams
- Current delivery methodology documentation (e.g., waterfall stage-gate, PMO charters, project governance policies)

## Optional Inputs
- Employee engagement or team health survey data indicating collaboration barriers
- Existing OKR or KPI frameworks in use across teams or business units

## Diagnostic Process
1. Assess the current organizational structure and delivery model, documenting span of control, decision-making latency, and handoff frequency between functions.
2. Identify value streams by mapping how customer value flows through the organization from request to delivery, and determine natural product or capability boundaries.
3. Define squad and tribe topology by grouping teams around value streams, assigning product ownership, and establishing mission statements for each unit.
4. Map current processes to agile equivalents, translating stage-gate reviews, steering committees, and project milestones into backlog refinement, sprint reviews, and quarterly planning.
5. Identify governance and decision-making changes required, including which decisions should be delegated to teams, which require portfolio-level escalation, and how budgeting shifts from project-based to product-based funding.
6. Define the transition roadmap, sequencing structural changes, capability-building interventions, and tooling adoption across a phased timeline with clear milestones.
7. Establish operating cadences — sprint planning, daily standup, sprint review, retrospective, and quarterly planning — with defined participants, inputs, and outputs for each ceremony.

## Output
The skill produces a structured agile operating model design including recommended team topology, governance changes, cadence map, and a phased transition roadmap. Findings are grounded in current-state diagnostics and calibrated to organizational readiness and regulatory constraints.
