# Agile Operating Model — References

## Foundational Works
- McKinsey & Company: *The Five Trademarks of Agile Organizations* (2018) — Empirical study identifying the structural and cultural characteristics common to high-performing agile enterprises, including network-of-teams architecture and rapid decision cycles
- Henrik Kniberg & Anders Ivarsson: *Scaling Agile @ Spotify* (2012) — Practitioner white paper introducing the squad, tribe, chapter, and guild model as a scalable agile topology for product organizations
- Scaled Agile Inc.: *Scaled Agile Framework (SAFe) 6.0* (2023) — Comprehensive enterprise agile framework covering team, program, and portfolio levels including PI Planning, ART structure, and Lean portfolio management

## Academic Sources
- Takeuchi, H. & Nonaka, I.: *The New New Product Development Game* (Harvard Business Review, 1986) — Original academic framing of cross-functional, self-organizing teams in product development that prefigures modern agile team design
- Rigby, D., Sutherland, J. & Takeuchi, H.: *Embracing Agile* (Harvard Business Review, 2016) — Research-backed analysis of conditions under which agile methods succeed or fail at the organizational level

## Practitioner Resources
- Atlassian Team Playbook: Structured workshop templates for agile team health checks, goal-setting, and retrospectives applicable to operating model transitions
- Deloitte Insights: *Organizational Agility* series — Advisory perspective on structuring agile transformations including budgeting, talent, and governance changes
- Project Management Institute: *Agile Practice Guide* (2017) — Co-published with Agile Alliance; provides practitioner guidance on selecting and tailoring agile methods for organizational context
