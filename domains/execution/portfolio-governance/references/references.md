# Portfolio Governance — References

## Foundational Works
- Project Management Institute: *The Standard for Portfolio Management*, 4th Edition (2017) — Defines the processes, knowledge areas and performance domains for portfolio management; establishes the authoritative taxonomy for portfolio component selection, prioritization, balancing and oversight that underpins modern portfolio governance practice
- Axelos: *Managing Successful Programmes (MSP)*, 5th Edition (2020) — Programme and portfolio governance framework widely adopted in the UK public sector and global enterprise; provides structured guidance on benefit realization management, risk aggregation and decision escalation at portfolio level

## Academic Sources
- Levine, Harvey A.: *Project Portfolio Management: A Practical Guide to Selecting Projects, Managing Portfolios, and Maximizing Benefits* (2005) — Provides academic and practitioner grounding for the portfolio selection, balancing and performance measurement disciplines; bridges theoretical portfolio optimization with practical governance implementation
- Archer, N.P. & Ghasemzadeh, F.: *An integrated framework for project portfolio selection*, International Journal of Project Management (1999) — Foundational academic paper on multi-criteria project portfolio selection; establishes the scoring and filtering methodology underlying impact-feasibility evaluation in portfolio governance

## Practitioner Resources
- Axelos: *Portfolio, Programme and Project Offices (P3O)* (2013) — Defines the structural and operational design of portfolio, programme and project offices; provides guidance on governance body design, reporting cadences and portfolio review facilitation
- Gartner: *IT Portfolio Management* — Practitioner research and advisory guidance on portfolio governance structures, technology tooling and reporting dashboards for CIO and IT leadership audiences
- Bain & Company: *Transformation with a capital T* — Practitioner guidance on governing large-scale transformation portfolios, including stop/start/continue decision frameworks and portfolio health diagnostic approaches
