# Process Mapping — References

## Foundational Works
- Michael Hammer & James Champy: *Reengineering the Corporation: A Manifesto for Business Revolution* (1993) — Introduced the concept of business process reengineering (BPR) — the fundamental rethinking and radical redesign of business processes to achieve dramatic improvements in performance; established the intellectual foundation for challenging existing process designs rather than incrementally optimizing them
- Geary Rummler & Alan Brache: *Improving Performance: How to Manage the White Space on the Organization Chart* (1995) — Introduced the swim lane diagram as the standard tool for mapping cross-functional processes; established the three levels of performance (organization, process, performer) and the concept that most performance problems occur in the handoffs between functions — the "white space" invisible on org charts

## Academic Sources
- Object Management Group: *Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN) 2.0 Standard* (2011) — The international standard for business process notation used in professional process mapping; defines the graphical elements, symbols and semantic rules governing formal process documentation and enabling interoperability between process design tools
- Davenport, Thomas H.: *Process Innovation: Reengineering Work through Information Technology* (1993) — Academic and practitioner treatment of process improvement methodologies; provides the analytical framework for evaluating which process changes deliver the highest performance improvement per implementation effort

## Practitioner Resources
- Lean Enterprise Institute: *Value Stream Mapping* methodology — Lean manufacturing-derived technique for mapping material and information flow through a production or service process; provides the waste identification taxonomy (Transportation, Inventory, Motion, Waiting, Overproduction, Overprocessing, Defects) widely applied in process mapping diagnostics
- APQC (American Productivity & Quality Center): *Process Classification Framework* — Industry-standard process taxonomy enabling benchmarking of process performance metrics against peer organizations; used to establish the external benchmark targets that motivate process improvement investments
- McKinsey & Company: *Digital Operations* — Practitioner guidance on integrating process mapping with automation and digital transformation; addresses the sequencing imperative of mapping and optimizing processes before applying RPA or AI tooling
