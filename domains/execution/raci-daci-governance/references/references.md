# RACI / DACI Governance — References

## Foundational Works
- Smith, M. & Erwin, J.: *Role & Responsibility Charting (RACI)* (2005) — The definitive practitioner guide to RACI matrix construction, common structural failure modes (multiple Accountable owners, missing Responsible roles, bottleneck Consulted assignments) and the facilitation methodology for stakeholder validation workshops
- Bain & Company: *RAPID Decision Framework* — Bain's proprietary decision rights model using Recommend, Agree, Perform, Input and Decide as the accountability categories; provides an alternative to RACI optimized for high-stakes strategic decisions where the distinction between input rights and decide rights is more consequential than process ownership

## Academic Sources
- Galbraith, Jay R.: *Designing Organizations: An Executive Guide to Strategy, Structure, and Process* (2002) — Provides the organizational design theory underlying accountability mapping; establishes the relationship between organizational structure, decision authority and lateral coordination mechanisms that RACI frameworks operationalize
- Mintzberg, Henry: *The Structuring of Organizations* (1979) — Foundational treatment of how organizations coordinate work through direct supervision, standardization and mutual adjustment; provides theoretical grounding for understanding when formal accountability frameworks like RACI add governance value versus when informal coordination is sufficient

## Practitioner Resources
- Project Management Institute: *A Guide to the Project Management Body of Knowledge (PMBOK Guide)*, 7th Edition (2021) — Includes responsibility assignment matrix (RAM) design as a core project governance tool; provides integration guidance for RACI within broader project governance and stakeholder management frameworks
- Kantar: *DACI Framework* — Practitioner documentation of the Decide, Advise, Commit, Inform variant; optimized for product management and cross-functional team environments where the distinction between advising and committing carries operational significance
- McKinsey & Company: *Untangling your organization's decision making* — Practitioner article documenting common decision accountability failures in large organizations and the diagnostic techniques used to identify and resolve them; includes empirical data on the correlation between decision clarity and organizational performance
