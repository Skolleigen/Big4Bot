# Initiative Prioritization Matrix — References

## Foundational Works
- Dwight D. Eisenhower: *Eisenhower Matrix* — The urgency/importance two-by-two framework, widely adapted as the foundational structure for impact-effort prioritization matrices in organizational settings
- Boston Consulting Group: *Prioritization Frameworks for Strategic Planning* — BCG advisory perspectives on portfolio prioritization, resource allocation trade-offs, and executive alignment in large-scale transformation programs
- Intercom Product Team: *RICE Scoring Model* (2016) — Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort scoring framework developed for product roadmap prioritization; widely adopted in technology and consulting contexts

## Academic Sources
- Cooper, R.G., Edgett, S.J. & Kleinschmidt, E.J.: *Portfolio Management for New Products* (2001) — Foundational research on scoring models and portfolio balancing techniques for R&D and product investment decisions
- Sull, D. & Eisenhardt, K.M.: *Simple Rules: How to Thrive in a Complex World* (2015) — Academic and practitioner perspective on how organizations can use heuristic rules to make faster, better prioritization decisions under uncertainty

## Practitioner Resources
- Project Management Institute: *The Standard for Portfolio Management, Fourth Edition* (2017) — Defines portfolio component categorization, scoring, and selection methods aligned to organizational strategy
- McKinsey & Company: *Strategic Initiative Prioritization* advisory series — Practitioner guidance on applying weighted scoring and capacity constraints to strategic initiative selection in corporate transformations
- Atlassian: *RICE Prioritization Framework* documentation — Implementation guidance and tooling for applying RICE scoring to product and initiative backlogs in agile environments
