# Scrum Framework — References

## Foundational Works
- Ken Schwaber & Jeff Sutherland: *The Scrum Guide* (2020 edition) — The authoritative and definitive description of the Scrum framework; maintained by the framework's co-creators and available free at scrumguides.org; defines the Scrum roles, artifacts, events and their purpose with intentional brevity to allow context-specific implementation
- Jeff Sutherland: *Scrum: The Art of Doing Twice the Work in Half the Time* (2014) — Sutherland's practitioner account of Scrum's origins, design principles and empirical performance improvements; provides the business case for Scrum adoption including the velocity improvement trajectories and stakeholder satisfaction data that characterize successful implementations

## Academic Sources
- Schwaber, Ken: *SCRUM Development Process*, Proceedings of the Workshop on Business Object Design and Implementation, OOPSLA (1995) — The original academic paper introducing Scrum as a software development methodology; documents the theoretical foundations in empirical process control theory (inspection, adaptation, transparency) that distinguish Scrum from plan-driven methods
- Takeuchi, Hirotaka & Nonaka, Ikujiro: *The New New Product Development Game*, Harvard Business Review (1986) — The foundational management research paper that inspired Scrum; identified the "rugby" approach to product development — cross-functional teams moving as a unit through overlapping development phases — as superior to the "relay race" sequential handoff model

## Practitioner Resources
- Scaled Agile Inc.: *Scaled Agile Framework (SAFe) 6.0* — The leading enterprise scaling framework for Scrum; provides guidance on coordinating multiple Scrum teams through Agile Release Trains, PI Planning and portfolio-level Agile governance for organizations operating Scrum at scale
- Mike Cohn (Mountain Goat Software): *Succeeding with Agile: Software Development Using Scrum* (2009) — Comprehensive practitioner guide covering backlog creation, estimation techniques (planning poker, story points), sprint planning facilitation and the organizational change management required to transition teams from waterfall to Scrum
- Scrum Alliance: *State of Scrum Report* — Annual practitioner survey documenting Scrum adoption patterns, ceremony adherence, velocity benchmarks and the organizational conditions associated with high-performing Scrum team implementations
