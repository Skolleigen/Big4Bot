# Employee Value Proposition — References

## Foundational Works
- Dave Ulrich & Wayne Brockbank: *The HR Value Proposition* (2005) — Establishes the strategic framework for HR as a value creator rather than an administrative function, including EVP design as a lever for talent strategy and business performance
- Towers Watson: *Employee Value Proposition Framework* — Practitioner model segmenting the employment deal into five dimensions (compensation, benefits, career, work environment, and company affiliation) with benchmarking methodology and activation guidance

## Academic Sources
- Frederick Herzberg: *One More Time: How Do You Motivate Employees?*, Harvard Business Review (1968) — Foundational research distinguishing hygiene factors (whose absence causes dissatisfaction) from motivators (whose presence drives engagement); directly applicable to EVP gap prioritization between threshold requirements and differentiators
- Mark Ehrhart, Benjamin Schneider & William Macey: *Organizational Climate and Culture* (2014) — Research on the relationship between perceived organizational practices and employee attitudes, providing the theoretical link between EVP elements and retention outcomes

## Practitioner Resources
- LinkedIn Talent Solutions: *Global Talent Trends* (annual report) — Large-scale survey research on candidate and employee priorities across geographies and industries; provides EVP benchmarking data on what talent segments value most in employer selection decisions
- Gartner HR: *Redesigning the Employee Value Proposition* — Research on how EVP effectiveness has shifted in the post-pandemic labor market, including data on the growing importance of flexibility, purpose, and manager quality relative to compensation and benefits
- Glassdoor Economic Research: *Employer Brand Statistics* — Quantitative research establishing the relationship between Glassdoor ratings, employer brand perception, and talent acquisition outcomes including offer acceptance rates and time-to-fill metrics
