# 360-Degree Feedback

## Domain
Organization & Culture

## Description
Multi-rater performance assessment tool collecting structured feedback on an individual from peers, direct reports, managers, and the individual themselves to identify development areas. It surfaces blind spots and provides a comprehensive view of behavioral competencies that single-source evaluations cannot capture.

## Purpose
360-degree feedback provides individuals with a calibrated, multi-perspective view of their strengths and development needs relative to a defined competency model. It supports leadership development, succession planning, and performance management by quantifying the gap between self-perception and how others experience an individual. Organizations use it to accelerate targeted development and build self-awareness at scale.

## When to Use
- A leadership development program requires objective, behavioral data to anchor individual coaching plans
- An organization is investing in succession planning and needs calibrated assessments of high-potential employees
- A team is experiencing interpersonal friction and structured multi-rater data is needed to surface root causes
- Post-transformation review requires evidence of whether new leadership behaviors have been adopted

## When NOT to Use
- The organization lacks a defined competency model against which to assess behavior
- Rater groups are too small (fewer than three raters per category) to ensure statistical anonymity
- The organizational culture does not yet support psychological safety needed for honest feedback
- The process is being deployed as a punitive or compensation-determination tool rather than a developmental one

## Inputs Required
- Defined competency model with behavioral indicators for the role or level being assessed
- List of ratees (individuals being assessed) with confirmed rater group assignments
- Survey instrument aligned to the competency model with a validated rating scale and open-text questions

## Optional Inputs
- Prior year 360 results for longitudinal trend comparison
- Role-specific performance data to contextualize behavioral ratings

## Diagnostic Process
1. Define the competency model and feedback dimensions relevant to the role, level, and organizational context; align with HR and business leadership on which behaviors are being assessed and how the scale will be interpreted.
2. Select and confirm rater groups for each ratee: self, direct manager, peers (minimum three), and direct reports (minimum three); brief all raters on the purpose, anonymity protocol, and expected timeline.
3. Administer the structured survey instrument through a secure platform with a two-week response window and automated reminders at days five and ten; monitor completion rates by rater group and follow up with low-response categories.
4. Aggregate ratings at the rater-group level and calculate the gap between self-perception scores and others' average ratings for each competency dimension; flag gaps exceeding one standard deviation as significant.
5. Identify the top three strengths (highest-rated competencies by others) and top three development gaps (largest negative self-to-others discrepancies or lowest absolute scores from others).
6. Produce a written feedback report for each ratee including competency score distributions, qualitative themes from open-text responses, and narrative interpretation; debrief the individual with a qualified coach or HR business partner within two weeks of delivery.
7. Co-design a 90-day individual development plan addressing the top two development gaps, specifying behavioral commitments, on-the-job practice opportunities, and a scheduled progress review checkpoint.

## Output
The skill produces a structured feedback report per ratee summarizing competency ratings by rater group, self-to-others gaps, key strengths, and prioritized development areas. When run across a leadership cohort, it produces an aggregate heat map identifying organization-wide capability strengths and systemic development needs.
