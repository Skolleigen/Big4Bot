# 360-Degree Feedback — References

## Foundational Works
- David Ward: *360 Degree Feedback* (1997) — Early practitioner guide establishing the structural requirements for multi-rater feedback including rater anonymity thresholds, scale design, and debrief protocols
- Marshall Goldsmith & Mark Reiter: *What Got You Here Won't Get You There* (2007) — Behavioral analysis of leadership derailment patterns most commonly surfaced through 360 feedback, with coaching implications for senior leaders

## Academic Sources
- Leanne Atwater & Joan Facteau: *Rater Agreement in Multi-Source Feedback*, Journal of Applied Psychology — Research establishing statistical criteria for significant self-to-others gaps and conditions under which multi-rater feedback predicts behavioral change
- David Antonioni: *Designing an Effective 360-Degree Appraisal Feedback Process*, Organizational Dynamics — Framework for aligning 360 instrument design with specific competency models and developmental versus evaluative purposes

## Practitioner Resources
- Center for Creative Leadership: *CCL 360 Assessment Methodology* — Practitioner standards for rater group composition, anonymity protocols, feedback report design, and facilitated debrief best practices
- Society for Human Resource Management: *SHRM 360-Degree Feedback Toolkit* — Implementation guidelines covering legal considerations, communication templates, and integration with performance management systems
- Korn Ferry: *Leadership Architect Competency Library* — Competency model reference used to anchor 360 behavioral rating scales against validated leadership dimensions
