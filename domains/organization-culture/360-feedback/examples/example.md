# 360-Degree Feedback — Example

## Scenario
A VP of Engineering at a 300-person technology company undergoes a 360-degree feedback process as part of the company's annual leadership development program. Raters include 4 peers, 6 direct reports, and the VP's direct manager. The competency framework covers 8 dimensions: strategic thinking, communication, team empowerment, decision-making, technical credibility, stakeholder management, talent development, and operational execution.

## Inputs
- **Ratee:** VP of Engineering (8 years tenure, recently promoted from Senior Director)
- **Rater groups:** 1 manager, 4 peers, 6 direct reports; self-assessment completed
- **Competency model:** 8-dimension leadership framework with 5-point behavioral rating scale
- **Self-rating on "Team Empowerment":** 4.2 / 5.0
- **Peer average on "Team Empowerment":** 3.1 / 5.0
- **Direct report average on "Team Empowerment":** 2.8 / 5.0
- **Manager rating on "Team Empowerment":** 3.4 / 5.0

## Analysis

**Step 1 — Competency model alignment:**
The 8-dimension framework is confirmed as appropriate for a VP of Engineering. "Team Empowerment" is anchored to behaviors including delegating decisions to appropriate levels, avoiding unnecessary approval requirements, and creating space for team members to resolve issues independently. All rater groups received behavioral anchor descriptions before completing ratings.

**Step 2 — Rater group confirmation and anonymity:**
All 11 raters completed the survey within the two-week window (100% response rate). With 4 peers and 6 direct reports, both groups meet the minimum anonymity threshold of 3 raters per category. Self-assessment was completed on day one of the window.

**Step 3 — Survey administration and aggregation:**
Ratings aggregated by rater group show strong cross-group alignment on Technical Credibility (self: 4.5, others avg: 4.4) and Strategic Thinking (self: 4.0, others avg: 3.9), confirming well-calibrated self-awareness in these areas. Communication also shows alignment (self: 3.8, others avg: 3.7).

**Step 4 — Gap identification:**
The largest self-to-others gap appears on "Team Empowerment": the VP self-rates 4.2 while peers average 3.1 (-1.1 gap) and direct reports average 2.8 (-1.4 gap). This gap exceeds 1.0 standard deviation and is flagged as a significant blind spot. The second-largest gap appears on "Talent Development" (self: 3.9, direct reports avg: 3.0, gap: -0.9). No dimension shows a reverse gap where others rate higher than self by more than 0.3 points.

**Step 5 — Strength and development area identification:**
Top three strengths rated by others: (1) Technical Credibility at 4.4, (2) Operational Execution at 4.1, (3) Strategic Thinking at 3.9. Top two development gaps: (1) Team Empowerment — the 1.4-point gap with direct reports is the largest discrepancy in the cohort and is supported by qualitative comments referencing approval bottlenecks, last-minute reversals of team decisions, and reluctance to accept solutions differing from the VP's own preference. (2) Talent Development — direct reports note infrequent structured development conversations and limited sponsorship for stretch assignments.

**Step 6 — Qualitative theme synthesis:**
Open-text responses from direct reports cluster around three themes: approval bottlenecks ("decisions get reversed after I've already executed"), ambiguity about autonomous action ("I'm not sure when I'm empowered to act"), and limited growth investment ("development conversations are mostly performance-focused, not career-focused"). Peer qualitative responses reference the VP as "detail-oriented to a fault in design reviews." Manager observation: "High performer technically, but needs to scale leadership through the team rather than direct involvement."

**Step 7 — Individual development plan co-design:**
A 90-day IDP is co-designed with the VP and their executive coach. Commitment 1: Implement a documented decision rights matrix for the engineering organization within 30 days specifying which decisions require VP approval versus team-level authority. Commitment 2: Conduct monthly structured development 1:1s with each direct report using a consistent template covering growth goals, sponsorship, and stretch opportunities. Progress review scheduled at day 45 and day 90.
