# Total Rewards Strategy

## Domain
Organization & Culture

## Description
A framework for designing and optimizing an organization's compensation, benefits, well-being programs, and career development initiatives. This skill translates the abstract guarantees of an Employee Value Proposition (EVP) into concrete, operationalized rewards structures that attract, motivate, and retain critical talent while adhering to financial constraints.

## Purpose
While an Employee Value Proposition (EVP) defines *why* a candidate should choose or stay at a company, the Total Rewards Strategy dictates *how* that value is systematically delivered. A poorly designed rewards structure can destroy a strong EVP by creating internal inequity, incentivizing the wrong behaviors, or causing the organization to overpay for easily replaceable skills while losing critical talent. This framework ensures reward spend aligns with strategic business goals.

## When to Use
- Translating a newly minted or refreshed Employee Value Proposition into actionable HR policy.
- Experiencing high turnover among critical talent cohorts despite "competitive" base salaries.
- Post-merger integration requiring the harmonization of two disparate compensation and benefits structures.
- Shifting organizational strategy (e.g., moving from a high-growth startup phase to mature profitability) requiring a shift from heavy equity compensation to cash and stability.

## When NOT to Use
- Handling individual, ad-hoc compensation grievances or off-cycle promotion requests.
- Formulating the overarching Employee Value Proposition (this framework operationalizes EVP, it doesn't define it).
- Conducting routine annual salary benchmarking without an intent to restructure.

## Inputs Required
- The organization's overarching Employee Value Proposition (EVP) and strategic talent goals.
- Current compensation and benefits spend (budget constraints).
- Employee demographics and feedback (e.g., from pulse-surveys) indicating current dissatisfaction or desired benefits.

## Optional Inputs
- Industry benchmarking data for compensation across relevant talent pools.
- Prior output from `360-feedback` indicating performance-to-reward disconnects.
- Known constraints (e.g., strict cash preservation requiring equity-heavy rewards).

## Diagnostic Process
1. **EVP & Strategy Alignment:** Review the business strategy and EVP to determine the desired talent behaviors (e.g., risk-taking innovation vs. steady operational excellence). 
2. **Current State Assessment:** Audit the existing mix of Total Rewards components: Compensation (base, variable, equity), Benefits (health, retirement), Well-being (flexibility, mental health), and Recognition (career development, awards).
3. **Internal Equity & External Competitiveness:** Evaluate pay parity across demographic groups and map current pay bands against external industry benchmarks to identify flight risks or overspending.
4. **Segmentation by Talent Value:** Differentiate reward structures based on talent segmentation. Allocate disproportionate variable reward budgets to roles that disproportionately drive strategic value.
5. **Mix Optimization:** Shift the total rewards distribution (e.g., higher base/lower variable vs. lower base/higher variable) to match the cultural intent defined by the EVP. Introduce low-cost/high-value non-financial rewards (like extreme flexibility) if cash is constrained.
6. **Program Design & Governance:** Define clear metric-driven triggers for variable compensation payouts and establish an annual review governance structure to prevent pay compression.

## Output
The analysis produces a segmented Total Rewards framework detailing the recommended mix of financial and non-financial rewards, a gap analysis against the current state, and specific programmatic recommendations designed to operationalize the desired EVP within budget constraints.
