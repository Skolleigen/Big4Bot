# References

- **WorldatWork (2006, Updated 2015).** *The WorldatWork Total Rewards Model*. WorldatWork Press.
  - The globally recognized industry standard model. It defines the six elements of total rewards (compensation, benefits, work-life effectiveness, recognition, performance management, and talent development) and how they must interlock to attract, motivate, and retain talent.

- **Ledford, G. E., & Heneman, H. G. (2011).** *Compensation: A Study of the Pay-Performance Connection*. Society for Human Resource Management (SHRM).
  - A foundational review on how variable pay and compensation structures must align with desired organizational behavior and strategic objectives rather than acting as a static entitlement.

- **Boudreau, J. W., & Ramstad, P. M. (2007).** *Beyond HR: The New Science of Human Capital*. Harvard Business School Press.
  - Emphasizes the need for "talentship"—applying the same rigorous segmentation and investment logic to human capital (total rewards) as organizations apply to financial capital or customer segmentation.
