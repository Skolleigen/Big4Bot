# Organizational Network Analysis — References

## Foundational Works
- Rob Cross & Andrew Parker: *The Hidden Power of Social Networks: Understanding How Work Really Gets Done in Organizations* (2004) — The defining practitioner text on ONA, establishing the methodology for advice, information, and energy network surveys; introduces the concepts of network stars, peripheral nodes, and overloaded connectors with intervention design guidance
- Rob Cross, Stephen Borgatti & Andrew Parker: *Making Invisible Work Visible: Using Social Network Analysis to Support Strategic Collaboration*, California Management Review (2002) — Foundational applied research demonstrating how ONA maps reveal the informal work patterns that formal org charts obscure; includes methodology for calculating centrality metrics and interpreting them for organizational intervention

## Academic Sources
- Ronald Burt: *Structural Holes: The Social Structure of Competition* (1992) — Foundational network theory establishing the concept of structural holes — gaps between clusters that brokers bridge — and providing the theoretical basis for betweenness centrality as a measure of brokerage advantage and organizational risk concentration
- Mark Granovetter: *The Strength of Weak Ties*, American Journal of Sociology (1973) — Classic research demonstrating that weak cross-cluster ties carry more novel information than strong within-cluster ties; provides the theoretical foundation for using ONA to diagnose silo conditions and design cross-cluster collaboration interventions
- David Krackhardt & Jeffrey Hanson: *Informal Networks: The Company Behind the Chart*, Harvard Business Review (1993) — Applied research mapping three types of organizational networks (advice, trust, communication) and demonstrating that performance and change adoption correlate with informal network position rather than formal hierarchical position

## Practitioner Resources
- IBM Institute for Business Value: *Social Business: Advent of a New Age* — IBM's organizational network analysis methodology applied to large-scale enterprise networks, including guidance on using communication metadata from collaboration platforms to triangulate with survey-based ONA data
- Deloitte Insights: *Organizational Network Analysis: Understanding Informal Collaboration* — Practitioner framework for integrating ONA into talent management, change management, and organizational design programs; includes case studies from financial services and professional services sectors
- MIT Sloan Management Review: *The New Science of Building Great Teams* (Pentland, 2012) — Research on communication patterns as predictors of team performance, using sensor-based collaboration data to complement survey-based ONA and establish the relationship between network patterns and business outcomes
