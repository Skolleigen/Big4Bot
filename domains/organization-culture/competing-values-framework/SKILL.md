# Competing Values Framework

## Domain
Organization & Culture

## Description
Organizational culture model classifying organizations along two axes — internal versus external focus and stability versus flexibility — producing four culture quadrants: Clan, Adhocracy, Market, and Hierarchy. It provides a diagnostic vocabulary for understanding current culture type and a structured method for planning culture change toward a desired state.

## Purpose
Organizations often struggle to articulate what their culture actually is, making it impossible to deliberately manage culture as a strategic asset. The Competing Values Framework provides a validated, quantifiable model that translates the abstract concept of organizational culture into four measurable archetypes with distinct leadership styles, success criteria, and management practices. Leaders use it to diagnose the gap between current and desired culture, prioritize culture change interventions, and track cultural evolution over time.

## When to Use
- A leadership team wants to understand the current culture type before embarking on a strategic transformation requiring different cultural behaviors
- Post-merger or acquisition integration requires assessing the cultural distance between two organizations before designing integration interventions
- Organizational performance issues (innovation deficit, excessive bureaucracy, high attrition, poor collaboration) suggest a cultural root cause requiring diagnosis
- The organization has completed a strategy refresh and leadership wants to assess whether the current culture will enable or impede execution of the new strategy

## When NOT to Use
- The organization needs an employee-level engagement or sentiment measure rather than a culture type diagnosis — use pulse-surveys or OCAI instead
- Leadership is not prepared to act on findings; culture assessment without follow-through creates cynicism and erodes trust in future diagnostic efforts
- The assessment population is fewer than 20 people, making quadrant score aggregation statistically unreliable
- The organization is in a period of active crisis where immediate stabilization takes precedence over culture diagnostic work

## Inputs Required
- CVF culture survey responses from a representative sample of leadership and employees (minimum 20 respondents for reliable scoring)
- Organizational context: industry, size, strategic priorities, and current performance challenges
- Defined desired future state or strategic ambition against which to assess cultural fit
- Segmentation data to enable analysis by level, function, or business unit if required

## Optional Inputs
- Prior CVF or OCAI assessment results for longitudinal comparison
- Competitive benchmarks or industry culture norms for external reference
- Leadership team facilitated discussion outputs on values and desired behaviors

## Diagnostic Process
1. Administer the CVF culture survey to the target population, ensuring representation across organizational levels and functions; brief respondents on the purpose and anonymity protocol to maximize response quality and rate.
2. Score the current culture profile by calculating average ratings for each of the four quadrants — Clan, Adhocracy, Market, and Hierarchy — across all respondents; segment results by level and function to identify intra-organizational culture variation.
3. Facilitate a leadership session to define the desired future culture profile using the same CVF quadrant scoring methodology; ensure the desired profile is anchored to specific strategic imperatives rather than abstract preferences.
4. Calculate the gap between the current and desired culture profile for each quadrant, identifying which quadrants require strengthening and which require reduction; gaps exceeding 10 percentage points are material and require prioritized intervention.
5. Identify the specific organizational practices, leadership behaviors, incentive structures, and management systems that are driving the current culture profile in each quadrant; distinguish between drivers that can be changed (interventions) and constraints that must be accommodated (boundary conditions).
6. Recommend cultural interventions targeted at the specific quadrant gaps: for Clan gap, redesign collaboration practices and recognition systems; for Adhocracy gap, create structured innovation mechanisms and psychological safety programs; for Market gap, sharpen external accountability and performance metrics; for Hierarchy gap, streamline governance and approval processes.
7. Define a culture measurement and progress review plan with a follow-up CVF assessment at 12–18 months, interim pulse survey indicators aligned to the target quadrant behaviors, and accountability owners for each intervention workstream.

## Output
The skill produces a culture profile report showing the current and desired quadrant scores plotted as radar charts, gap analysis by quadrant and organizational segment, identified culture drivers and inhibitors, and a prioritized set of cultural interventions with accountable owners. When delivered across business units, it enables a culture heat map showing cultural variance across the organization.
