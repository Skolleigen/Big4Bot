# Culture Workshops — References

## Foundational Works
- Edgar H. Schein: *Organizational Culture and Leadership* (1985, 5th ed. 2016) — Foundational framework establishing culture as operating at three levels (artifacts, espoused values, and underlying assumptions) and providing the analytical basis for workshop activities designed to surface tacit norms that formal communication cannot reach
- Patrick Lencioni: *The Five Dysfunctions of a Team* (2002) — Practitioner model for diagnosing team-level cultural dysfunction across five dimensions (absence of trust, fear of conflict, lack of commitment, avoidance of accountability, inattention to results); widely used as a diagnostic lens in team culture workshops

## Academic Sources
- Amy Edmondson: *Psychological Safety and Learning Behavior in Work Teams*, Administrative Science Quarterly (1999) — Foundational research establishing the conditions under which group members will engage honestly in culture dialogue; directly informs workshop design decisions around facilitation, participant selection, and session framing
- Karl Weick: *Sensemaking in Organizations* (1995) — Theoretical framework explaining how groups construct shared meaning from ambiguous situations; provides the academic basis for using storytelling and narrative methods in culture workshops to surface and shift collective understanding

## Practitioner Resources
- IDEO: *The Field Guide to Human-Centered Design* — Facilitation methodology used to structure empathy-building and co-creation activities in culture workshops; the ideation and prototyping phases map directly to desired-state culture design exercises
- Liberating Structures: *Liberating Structures Facilitation Repertoire* — Collection of 33 facilitation microstructures (including 1-2-4-All, TRIZ, and Min Specs) designed to enable genuine participation across hierarchical levels; widely applied in large-group culture workshop designs to replace presentation-and-discussion formats
- Organization Development Network (ODN): *OD Practitioner Journal* — Peer-reviewed practitioner resource covering facilitation methodology, culture intervention design, and evaluation approaches for organization development initiatives including culture workshops
