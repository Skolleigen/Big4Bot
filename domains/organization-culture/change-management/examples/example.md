# Change Management — Example

## Scenario
A 2,000-employee insurance company is migrating its claims processing operations from a legacy on-premise system to a cloud-based platform over a 6-month implementation window. Approximately 400 claims handlers, 40 team leaders, and 12 operations managers are directly impacted. A pre-launch readiness assessment reveals that only 32% of affected employees feel confident in using the new system, and qualitative interviews surface two dominant concerns: fear that automation features will make their roles redundant, and anxiety about the complexity of new workflows relative to the familiar legacy system.

## Inputs
- **Organization:** 2,000-employee property and casualty insurance company
- **Change:** Legacy claims processing system migration to cloud platform; go-live in 6 months
- **Affected population:** 400 claims handlers, 40 team leaders, 12 operations managers
- **Readiness baseline:** 32% of impacted employees feel confident in the new system (target: 75% by go-live)
- **Primary resistance sources:** Job redundancy fears (cited by 58% of claims handlers); workflow complexity concerns (cited by 71%)
- **Sponsor:** Chief Operations Officer with visible commitment; line managers rated as moderate change ambassadors

## Analysis

**Step 1 — Change scope and business case definition:**
The change scope covers replacement of the legacy ClaimsEdge system with CloudClaims Pro across all personal lines claims handling. The business case projects a 22% reduction in average claims handling time, a 15% reduction in error rates, and annual cost savings of $3.2M. The future state requires claims handlers to manage cases through a browser-based interface with automated triage, digital document management, and real-time supervisor dashboards replacing manual status reporting. The scope boundary is confirmed as the 452 impacted employees in the Claims Operations division; IT implementation and vendor management are managed separately.

**Step 2 — Organizational readiness assessment:**
A 12-question readiness survey administered to all 452 impacted employees yields 89% response rate. Key findings: 32% feel confident in the new system (target: 75% by go-live), 44% are aware of the go-live date, 61% have heard about the change but cannot describe what specifically changes in their role, and 58% express concern about job security. Team leaders score highest on awareness (67%) and lowest on confidence in coaching their teams through the transition (29%). The lowest-readiness segment is the 220 handlers in the Commercial Lines team, where only 18% express confidence.

**Step 3 — Stakeholder segmentation:**
Stakeholders segmented by impact and influence: (1) Claims Handlers — high impact, low influence; primary audience for communication and training. (2) Team Leaders — high impact, high influence; critical cascade channel and coaching resource requiring investment. (3) Operations Managers — high impact, high influence; must model new behaviors and provide visible endorsement. (4) HR Business Partners — low impact, high influence; enabler of manager support and escalation handling. (5) IT Implementation Team — moderate impact, moderate influence; technical dependency requiring coordination on training environment readiness. Two team leaders in the Personal Lines unit are identified as informal influencers with peer credibility; recruited as Change Champions.

**Step 4 — Communication plan:**
A four-channel communication plan is designed: (a) COO town hall at month 1 addressing job security directly with a "no redundancy" commitment and explaining the rationale for migration; recorded and distributed to all shifts. (b) Manager cascade toolkit deployed monthly to team leaders with talking points, FAQ documents, and suggested team meeting agendas. (c) Intranet hub "Claims Transformation" launched with system overview, timeline, training schedule, and FAQ; updated biweekly. (d) Peer Champion network of 8 volunteers communicates informally and surfaces questions for escalation. All communications lead with "what this means for you" framing before providing technical details.

**Step 5 — Training and capability-building program:**
Three-tier training program designed: (a) Awareness tier — all 452 employees complete a 45-minute self-paced module on the new system's purpose, interface overview, and key workflow changes by month 2. (b) Role-specific procedural tier — claims handlers complete 8 hours of hands-on training in a sandboxed environment across months 3–4, segmented by claim type (personal lines, commercial lines, specialty); training validated by a simulated case completion assessment with 85% pass threshold. (c) Leader enablement tier — team leaders and operations managers complete a 4-hour "coaching through change" workshop in month 2 equipping them to handle resistance conversations and run effective team check-ins.

**Step 6 — Resistance identification and mitigation:**
Two primary resistance sources require targeted mitigation: (1) Job redundancy fears — mitigated by COO commitment at town hall, a written FAQ addressing automation scope published on the intranet, and a series of "day in the life of a claims handler post-go-live" video testimonials from pilot team participants. No role eliminations are planned; two new Quality Assurance roles are being created and posted internally first, providing a visible counternarrative. (2) Workflow complexity concerns — mitigated by extending the training sandbox access from 4 weeks to 8 weeks, deploying printed role-specific quick reference cards at every workstation on go-live day, and establishing a "first 30 days" help desk with a 4-hour response SLA staffed by the Change Champion network.

**Step 7 — Adoption monitoring:**
A biweekly adoption dashboard tracks: system login rates by team (target: 95% by week 2 post go-live), case processing completion rates in CloudClaims Pro versus legacy workarounds (target: 0% legacy use by day 30), training completion rates by tier and segment (target: 100% role-specific training before go-live), and pulse survey score on "I feel confident using the new system" (target: 75% positive by go-live). Commercial Lines team is flagged for weekly check-ins given lowest baseline confidence. Results reviewed at biweekly steering committee; interventions triggered when any metric falls more than 10 percentage points below target.
