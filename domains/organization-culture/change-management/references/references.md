# Change Management — References

## Foundational Works
- John P. Kotter: *Leading Change* (1996) — Establishes the 8-step model for large-scale organizational transformation, emphasizing urgency creation, coalition building, and short-term wins as prerequisites for sustained adoption
- William Bridges: *Managing Transitions: Making the Most of Change* (2009) — Distinguishes between the external change event and the internal psychological transition process, providing a framework for managing the neutral zone between old and new ways of working
- Daryl Conner: *Managing at the Speed of Change* (1993) — Introduces the concept of change saturation and the resilience model for assessing organizational capacity to absorb simultaneous change initiatives

## Academic Sources
- Jeffrey Hiatt: *ADKAR: A Model for Change in Business, Government and Our Community*, Prosci Research (2006) — Defines the Prosci ADKAR model (Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, Reinforcement) as a five-stage individual change adoption framework with diagnostic and intervention implications
- Kathleen Eisenhardt & Jason Furr: *Simple Rules for a Complex World*, Harvard Business Review — Research on how organizations with high change velocity benefit from simplified decision rules during transitions to reduce cognitive load

## Practitioner Resources
- Prosci: *Best Practices in Change Management* (annual benchmarking report) — Longitudinal research across thousands of change initiatives identifying the top correlates of adoption success, including active sponsorship, structured resistance management, and employee-facing communication frequency
- McKinsey & Company: *The McKinsey 7-S Framework* — Practitioner model for assessing organizational alignment across strategy, structure, systems, shared values, skills, staff, and style during change; useful for diagnosing second-order impacts of a primary change initiative
- Association of Change Management Professionals (ACMP): *Standard for Change Management* — Professional body standard defining competencies, practices, and ethical guidelines for practitioners leading organizational change programs
